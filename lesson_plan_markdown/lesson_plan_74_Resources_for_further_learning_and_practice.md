# Leveraging Resources for Continuous Learning in Wardley Mapping

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, entrepreneurs, and business strategists

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Evaluate and select appropriate resources for ongoing learning in Wardley Mapping | Evaluate |
| Design a personalized action plan for implementing Wardley Mapping in their startup | Create |
| Analyze the Wardley Mapping ecosystem to identify opportunities for professional growth | Analyze |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Strategic thinking
* Continuous learning
* Professional development
* Resource evaluation
* Action planning

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of Wardley Mapping concepts
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* Experience in strategic planning

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Digital whiteboard tool (e.g., Miro, Mural)
* Access to OnlineWardleyMaps tool
* Printed resource guide handouts

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a case study of a startup that successfully implemented Wardley Mapping through continuous learning. Facilitate a discussion on the challenges and opportunities in ongoing strategic development.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in discussion, sharing personal experiences with strategic planning challenges.

**Resources Used:** Case study presentation, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide multiple examples catering to different industry sectors

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather learner perspectives

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a structured exploration of various Wardley Mapping resources, including online courses, communities, and tools.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, investigate assigned resource categories and prepare brief presentations on their findings.

**Resources Used:** Resource guide handouts, curated list of Wardley Mapping websites and tools

**Differentiation:** Assign resource categories based on learners' experience levels and interests

**Technology Integration:** Utilize collaborative document editing for group note-taking and presentation preparation

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations on resource findings. Provide additional context and insights on the Wardley Mapping ecosystem.

**Learner Activities:** Present group findings, engage in Q&A, and take notes on other groups' presentations.

**Resources Used:** Digital whiteboard for visual mapping of the resource ecosystem

**Differentiation:** Encourage peer teaching and allow for varied presentation formats

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing for presentations and interactive whiteboard for ecosystem mapping

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through the process of creating a personalized action plan for implementing Wardley Mapping in their startups.

**Learner Activities:** Develop individual action plans, incorporating selected resources and setting specific goals for Wardley Mapping implementation.

**Resources Used:** Action plan template, OnlineWardleyMaps tool

**Differentiation:** Provide one-on-one coaching for learners needing additional support

**Technology Integration:** Use OnlineWardleyMaps for creating draft maps as part of the action plan

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate peer review of action plans and lead a reflection discussion on the learning process.

**Learner Activities:** Share action plans with peers for feedback, reflect on the learning experience and next steps.

**Resources Used:** Peer review rubric, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Offer alternative methods for sharing and receiving feedback (written, verbal, or visual)

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms for peer review sessions in virtual settings

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and presentations during Explore and Explain phases
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to evaluate and analyze Wardley Mapping resources
* **Summative**: Evaluation of personalized action plans for Wardley Mapping implementation
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create a strategic plan and apply learning to professional context

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice Wardley Mappers**: Provide additional scaffolding in resource exploration, focus on foundational resources and basic implementation strategies
* **Experienced Wardley Mappers**: Encourage exploration of advanced resources, focus on innovative applications and industry-specific adaptations

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How will incorporating Wardley Mapping change your approach to strategic planning?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing your action plan, and how will you address them?
* How can you leverage the Wardley Mapping community to support your ongoing learning?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did the lesson cater to the diverse experience levels of the learners?
* What aspects of the Wardley Mapping ecosystem seemed to resonate most with the learners?
* How can the action planning process be improved to better support implementation?
