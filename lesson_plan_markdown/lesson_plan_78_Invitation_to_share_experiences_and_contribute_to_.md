# Empowering Innovation: Contributing to the Wardley Mapping Community

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, entrepreneurs, and business strategists familiar with Wardley Mapping

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the importance of community contribution in the evolution of Wardley Mapping | Analyzing |
| Evaluate personal experiences with Wardley Mapping for potential contributions to the field | Evaluating |
| Create a plan for sharing Wardley Mapping insights and experiences within the professional community | Creating |
| Design innovative applications of Wardley Mapping in startup contexts | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Community contribution
* Knowledge sharing
* Innovation in strategic thinking
* Mentorship
* Academic collaboration
* Standardization
* Best practices
* Cross-sector application

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of Wardley Mapping principles
* Experience applying Wardley Mapping in a startup context
* Familiarity with strategic planning concepts

## Materials Needed
* Digital whiteboard or collaborative mapping tool
* Case studies of Wardley Mapping applications
* Access to online Wardley Mapping communities and resources
* Laptops or tablets for participants

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of the Wardley Mapping community's evolution and impact. Facilitate a discussion on participants' experiences with Wardley Mapping.

**Learner Activities:** Share personal experiences and insights gained from using Wardley Mapping in their startups.

**Resources Used:** Digital presentation, interactive polling tool

**Differentiation:** Provide options for verbal or written sharing to accommodate different communication preferences.

**Technology Integration:** Use an online collaborative board for participants to post their experiences and insights.

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through an exploration of various ways to contribute to the Wardley Mapping community. Present case studies of impactful contributions.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, analyze case studies and discuss potential areas for personal contribution based on their experiences.

**Resources Used:** Case study documents, collaborative discussion platform

**Differentiation:** Provide case studies of varying complexity to match participants' experience levels.

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms in video conferencing software for small group discussions.

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed information on methods for contributing to the field, including documentation, sharing, participation in events, and collaboration. Discuss the importance of innovation and pushing boundaries in Wardley Mapping.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and participate in discussions on how these contribution methods apply to their specific contexts.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, interactive Q&A platform

**Differentiation:** Offer multiple examples of contribution methods to cater to diverse professional backgrounds.

**Technology Integration:** Use a live Q&A tool to address participants' questions in real-time.

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop where participants develop plans for contributing to the Wardley Mapping community. Provide guidance on creating actionable steps.

**Learner Activities:** Create a personal action plan for contributing to the Wardley Mapping community, including specific goals and timelines.

**Resources Used:** Action plan templates, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one coaching for participants who need additional support in developing their plans.

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital project management tool to help participants structure their action plans.

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through a reflection process on their learning and plans for contribution. Facilitate peer feedback on action plans.

**Learner Activities:** Present their action plans to peers, give and receive feedback, and refine their plans based on input.

**Resources Used:** Peer evaluation rubrics, self-reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written) to accommodate different strengths.

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital feedback tool for structured peer evaluations.

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during group discussions and activities
  - Alignment: Assesses participants' engagement and understanding of contribution methods
* **Summative**: Evaluation of participants' action plans for contributing to the Wardley Mapping community
  - Alignment: Directly assesses the ability to create a plan for sharing insights and experiences

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice Wardley Mapping practitioners**: Provide additional resources and simplified case studies. Pair with more experienced participants for peer learning.
* **Experienced Wardley Mapping practitioners**: Offer advanced challenges, such as designing novel applications or mentoring opportunities.

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Explore applications of Wardley Mapping in fields such as public policy, academia, and non-profit sectors
* Discuss integration of Wardley Mapping with other strategic planning methodologies

## Real-World Applications
* Developing thought leadership in strategic planning
* Enhancing startup pitches and investor presentations with Wardley Maps
* Collaborating on cross-industry innovation projects
* Contributing to open-source Wardley Mapping tools and resources

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflect on personal growth in strategic thinking since adopting Wardley Mapping
* Identify areas of expertise and potential blind spots in Wardley Mapping practice
* Consider how contributing to the community can enhance professional development

## Extension Activities
* Participate in or organize a local Wardley Mapping meetup
* Write a blog post or article about a unique application of Wardley Mapping
* Develop a case study based on a successful Wardley Mapping implementation in their startup

## Safety Considerations
* Remind participants about confidentiality when sharing business strategies
* Discuss ethical considerations in sharing and using Wardley Maps

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How has your understanding of Wardley Mapping's community aspect evolved through this session?
* What unique perspectives or experiences do you believe you can contribute to the field?
* How might contributing to the Wardley Mapping community benefit your professional growth and your startup?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did the session encourage participants to consider community contribution?
* What aspects of the lesson seemed to resonate most with participants?
* How can future sessions be improved to better inspire and equip professionals to contribute to the field?
