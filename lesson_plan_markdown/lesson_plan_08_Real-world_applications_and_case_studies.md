# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Strategic Innovation in Startups

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, executives, and strategists in the government and public sector

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze complex business ecosystems using Wardley Mapping techniques | Analyzing |
| Evaluate market opportunities and resource allocation strategies using Wardley Maps | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map to communicate startup strategy to stakeholders | Creating |
| Design innovative solutions for established sectors using insights from Wardley Mapping | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Value Chain Visualization
* Market Opportunity Identification
* Resource Optimization
* Stakeholder Communication
* Disruptive Innovation
* Strategic Decision-making

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of government and public sector operations

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaborative platform
* Wardley Mapping software or templates
* Case study materials
* Laptops or tablets for participants

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world startup challenge in the public sector. Introduce Wardley Mapping as a strategic tool.

**Learner Activities:** Discuss current approaches to strategic planning and their limitations. Share experiences with strategic challenges.

**Resources Used:** Slide presentation, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Use varied examples to cater to different industry experiences

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software for real-time engagement

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the basics of creating a Wardley Map using a simplified example.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create a basic Wardley Map for a hypothetical startup scenario.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping tutorial, practice scenarios

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support for those new to visual mapping techniques

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tools for group work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed case studies showcasing successful applications of Wardley Mapping in startup contexts.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze case studies, identifying key insights and strategic decisions enabled by Wardley Mapping.

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, analysis worksheets

**Differentiation:** Offer case studies from various sectors to align with diverse participant backgrounds

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual breakout rooms for small group discussions

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop where participants apply Wardley Mapping to their own startup challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Create a Wardley Map for their own startup or a provided complex scenario, focusing on identifying opportunities and optimizing resources.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, strategic planning templates

**Differentiation:** Provide one-on-one coaching for participants struggling with complex scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing for real-time feedback on participant maps

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants in presenting their Wardley Maps and strategic insights to the group.

**Learner Activities:** Present Wardley Maps, explain strategic insights, and receive peer feedback.

**Resources Used:** Presentation guidelines, peer evaluation rubrics

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written) to accommodate different communication styles

**Technology Integration:** Use digital portfolios for participants to showcase their work

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and Wardley Map creation process
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Map and strategic recommendations
  - Alignment: Measures ability to create and interpret Wardley Maps for strategic decision-making

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Experienced strategists**: Provide more complex scenarios and encourage deeper analysis of ecosystem dynamics
* **Novice entrepreneurs**: Offer additional guidance on basic strategic concepts and simplified mapping exercises
