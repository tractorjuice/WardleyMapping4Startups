# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Successful Disruption: Lessons and Patterns

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, innovation managers, and business strategists

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze common patterns of successful disruption using Wardley Mapping | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of Wardley Mapping in identifying disruptive opportunities | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map to visualize potential disruptive strategies for a given industry | Creating |
| Synthesize lessons learned from disruptive startups to inform strategic decision-making | Synthesizing |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Disruptive innovation
* Value chain analysis
* Ecosystem thinking
* Strategic alignment
* Market evolution
* Rapid iteration
* Category creation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of technology trends

## Materials Needed
* Wardley Mapping software or large paper sheets and markers
* Case studies of disruptive startups
* Laptops or tablets for research and collaborative work
* Projector and screen for presentations

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of a recent disruptive startup success story. Facilitate a discussion on what made the startup successful and how they might have used strategic tools like Wardley Mapping.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in discussion, sharing insights from their own experiences with disruption or strategic planning.

**Resources Used:** Slide deck with case study, whiteboard for capturing ideas

**Differentiation:** Encourage participants to draw from their diverse industry backgrounds when contributing to the discussion.

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital whiteboard (e.g., Miro) for collaborative idea sharing.

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce the key concepts of Wardley Mapping and its application in identifying disruptive opportunities. Guide learners through a sample Wardley Map analysis of a disruptive startup.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, analyze provided case studies of disruptive startups, identifying common patterns and creating initial Wardley Maps.

**Resources Used:** Handouts with Wardley Mapping guidelines, case study materials

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support or advanced challenges based on learners' familiarity with strategic mapping tools.

**Technology Integration:** Use Wardley Mapping software for interactive demonstrations and group work.

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Lead a discussion on the common patterns of successful disruption identified in the case studies. Explain how Wardley Mapping can be used to visualize and leverage these patterns.

**Learner Activities:** Share findings from group work, discuss insights, and ask questions about applying Wardley Mapping to their own contexts.

**Resources Used:** Projector for group presentations, collaborative document for capturing insights

**Differentiation:** Encourage peer teaching, allowing more experienced participants to share their insights.

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative platform (e.g., Google Docs) to compile and organize class insights.

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce a challenge scenario where learners must apply Wardley Mapping to identify potential disruptive opportunities in a given industry. Provide guidance and feedback as needed.

**Learner Activities:** Work in teams to create a comprehensive Wardley Map for the challenge scenario, identifying potential disruptive strategies and presenting their findings.

**Resources Used:** Challenge scenario descriptions, Wardley Mapping tools

**Differentiation:** Offer different industry scenarios to align with learners' backgrounds and interests.

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms in video conferencing software for team collaboration, digital presentation tools for final presentations.

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a peer review session of the Wardley Maps and strategies developed by each team. Lead a reflective discussion on the lessons learned and their application to participants' own professional contexts.

**Learner Activities:** Present team strategies, provide constructive feedback to peers, and reflect on personal learnings and applications.

**Resources Used:** Rubric for evaluating Wardley Maps and disruptive strategies

**Differentiation:** Encourage self-assessment and personalized goal-setting for applying learnings.

**Technology Integration:** Use online survey tools for gathering anonymous feedback and assessing learning outcomes.

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and Wardley Mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to analyze and apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final team presentations and Wardley Maps
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to synthesize learnings and create strategic disruption plans
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on applying lessons learned to own professional context
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to transfer knowledge to real-world scenarios

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide additional resources on basic strategy concepts, offer more structured templates for Wardley Mapping exercises
* **Experienced innovators**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage mentoring of less experienced peers, provide opportunities for deeper analysis
