# Anticipating Future Trends and Disruptions with Wardley Mapping

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, executives, and strategic planners

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a business ecosystem using Wardley Mapping to identify potential future trends and disruptions | Analyzing |
| Evaluate weak signals in a Wardley Map to anticipate emerging technologies and market shifts | Evaluating |
| Create a strategy for a startup based on insights derived from a Wardley Map | Creating |
| Apply best practices for using Wardley Mapping in ongoing trend anticipation and strategic planning | Applying |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Weak signals
* Technological evolution
* Market shifts
* Ecosystem changes
* Disruptive opportunities
* Strategic positioning

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General awareness of current technology trends

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or large sheets of paper and markers
* Case study materials
* Handouts on Wardley Mapping principles

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief case study of a successful startup that anticipated a major market shift. Facilitate a discussion on how they might have foreseen this shift.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in discussion, sharing experiences of unexpected market changes in their industries.

**Resources Used:** Case study presentation, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with the case study industry

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative digital whiteboard for brainstorming ideas

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce Wardley Mapping concepts and guide learners through creating a basic map of their own business ecosystem.

**Learner Activities:** Create individual Wardley Maps for their startups or a provided scenario.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping tutorial, mapping software or paper/markers

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one assistance for those new to Wardley Mapping

**Technology Integration:** Use Wardley Mapping software with built-in tutorials

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Demonstrate how to identify weak signals, map technological evolution, and anticipate market shifts using Wardley Maps.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze sample Wardley Maps to practice identifying trends and potential disruptions.

**Resources Used:** Sample Wardley Maps, analysis worksheet

**Differentiation:** Provide more complex maps for experienced strategists

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive Wardley Map visualizations

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 75 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners in applying Wardley Mapping to their own startups or case studies to develop strategic insights.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to create detailed Wardley Maps, identify potential disruptions, and develop strategic recommendations.

**Resources Used:** Group work guidelines, strategic planning template

**Differentiation:** Assign roles within groups based on experience levels

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative strategy mapping tools

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations of strategic insights and provide feedback.

**Learner Activities:** Present group findings, participate in peer evaluation, and reflect on learning.

**Resources Used:** Presentation rubric, peer evaluation forms

**Differentiation:** Offer alternative presentation formats (e.g., written report, video)

**Technology Integration:** Use digital presentation tools with real-time feedback features

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of Wardley Map creation and analysis during Explore and Elaborate phases
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Group presentation of strategic insights derived from Wardley Mapping
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to synthesize learning into actionable business strategies
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on applying Wardley Mapping to their startup
  - Alignment: Assesses deep understanding and ability to apply concepts to specific business contexts

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide simplified Wardley Map templates, offer additional guidance on map creation
* **Experienced executives**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage mentoring of less experienced participants
* **Technical founders**: Focus on deep dives into technological evolution aspects of Wardley Mapping
* **Non-technical founders**: Emphasize market and ecosystem aspects, provide additional tech trend resources

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Connect to financial forecasting and investment strategies
* Explore links with product development and innovation management
* Discuss implications for human resources and talent acquisition

## Real-World Applications
* Using Wardley Mapping for pitch deck creation and investor presentations
* Applying insights to product roadmap development
* Informing partnership and acquisition strategies

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflect on how Wardley Mapping changes their approach to strategic thinking
* Discuss challenges in anticipating future trends and how to overcome them
* Consider how their perception of their business ecosystem has evolved

## Extension Activities
* Create a 5-year strategic plan based on Wardley Mapping insights
* Conduct a Wardley Mapping exercise with their entire team
* Develop a trend monitoring system based on weak signals identified

## Safety Considerations
* Remind participants about confidentiality when discussing business strategies
* Advise on ethical considerations in strategic planning and disruption

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How has Wardley Mapping changed your perspective on your business's future?
* What were the most challenging aspects of creating and interpreting Wardley Maps?
* How do you plan to incorporate Wardley Mapping into your regular strategic planning?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did participants grasp the concept of weak signals?
* Were the group activities successful in promoting collaborative learning?
* What aspects of Wardley Mapping seemed most valuable to this group of learners?
