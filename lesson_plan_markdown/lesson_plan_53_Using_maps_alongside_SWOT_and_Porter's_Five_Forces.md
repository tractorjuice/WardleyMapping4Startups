# Integrating Wardley Mapping with SWOT and Porter's Five Forces for Strategic Business Analysis

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Business strategists, startup founders, and corporate innovation teams

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Synthesize Wardley Mapping with SWOT analysis and Porter's Five Forces to create comprehensive strategic plans | Create |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of integrating multiple strategic tools for startup decision-making | Evaluate |
| Apply integrated strategic analysis to real-world business scenarios | Apply |
| Analyze how the integration of strategic tools enhances dynamic understanding of business environments | Analyze |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* SWOT Analysis
* Porter's Five Forces
* Strategic Integration
* Dynamic Strategy
* Value Chain Analysis
* Competitive Advantage
* Market Evolution

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of Wardley Mapping
* Familiarity with SWOT analysis
* Knowledge of Porter's Five Forces framework
* General business strategy concepts

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaboration tool
* Wardley Mapping templates
* SWOT analysis worksheets
* Porter's Five Forces templates
* Case study materials
* Laptops or tablets for participants

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world business challenge that requires comprehensive strategic analysis. Facilitate a brief discussion on the limitations of using single strategic tools.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in group discussion, sharing experiences with strategic analysis tools and their limitations.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Use varied examples from different industries to engage diverse professional backgrounds

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather initial thoughts on strategic tool integration

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through a review of Wardley Mapping, SWOT analysis, and Porter's Five Forces. Demonstrate initial connections between the tools.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create individual Wardley Maps, SWOT analyses, and Porter's Five Forces diagrams for a given business scenario.

**Resources Used:** Tool templates, scenario descriptions

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support materials for those less familiar with certain tools

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online whiteboards for group work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present the integrated approach, explaining how to overlay SWOT and Porter's Five Forces onto a Wardley Map. Discuss the benefits and challenges of this integration.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and begin to conceptualize how to apply the integrated approach to their group's scenario.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, example integrated maps

**Differentiation:** Offer visual and verbal explanations to cater to different learning styles

**Technology Integration:** Use animated slides to show the process of integration step-by-step

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group work on applying the integrated approach to their scenarios. Provide guidance and feedback as needed.

**Learner Activities:** Work in groups to create an integrated strategic analysis, combining Wardley Mapping with SWOT and Porter's Five Forces for their given scenario.

**Resources Used:** Worksheets, digital collaboration tools

**Differentiation:** Provide additional challenges for advanced groups, such as considering multiple future scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use project management tools to help groups organize their analysis process

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide groups in presenting their integrated analyses. Facilitate peer feedback and discussion on the effectiveness of the integrated approach.

**Learner Activities:** Present group work, provide constructive feedback to peers, reflect on the learning process and potential applications in their own work.

**Resources Used:** Presentation tools, feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., visual, verbal, written) to accommodate different strengths

**Technology Integration:** Use digital feedback tools for real-time peer evaluation

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group work and discussions throughout the lesson
  - Alignment: Assesses ongoing understanding and application of concepts
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final integrated strategic analysis presentations
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to synthesize tools and apply to real-world scenarios
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on the benefits and challenges of the integrated approach
  - Alignment: Evaluates critical thinking and ability to connect learning to professional context

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide additional explanations of basic concepts, offer simplified scenarios for practice
* **Experienced professionals**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage leadership roles in group work, provide opportunities for sharing expertise

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections

* Marketing: Explore how the integrated approach can enhance market positioning strategies
* Finance: Consider how the analysis can inform investment decisions and resource allocation
