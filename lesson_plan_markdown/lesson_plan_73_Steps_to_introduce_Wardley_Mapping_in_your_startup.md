# Implementing Wardley Mapping in Your Startup: A Strategic Approach

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, executives, and strategic decision-makers

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the key steps for introducing Wardley Mapping in a startup environment | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the potential impact of Wardley Mapping on startup strategy and innovation | Evaluating |
| Design an action plan for implementing Wardley Mapping in their own startup | Creating |
| Justify the importance of leadership buy-in and continuous mapping culture | Evaluating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Strategic decision-making
* Innovation in startups
* Leadership buy-in
* Pilot projects
* Internal expertise development
* Process integration
* Continuous mapping culture
* Technology leverage
* Impact measurement

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of startup operations
* Familiarity with strategic planning concepts
* General knowledge of business analysis tools

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Digital whiteboard or collaborative mapping tool
* Printed handouts of sample Wardley Maps
* Case study materials
* Post-it notes and markers

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of Wardley Mapping and its potential impact on startups. Share a compelling case study of a startup that successfully implemented Wardley Mapping.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a think-pair-share activity to discuss current strategic challenges in their startups that Wardley Mapping might address.

**Resources Used:** PowerPoint presentation, case study handout

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those unfamiliar with Wardley Mapping basics

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather and display learners' current strategic challenges

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a hands-on exploration of the seven steps to introduce Wardley Mapping, using a fictional startup scenario.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, analyze each step and discuss potential challenges and benefits for their own startups. Create a draft implementation timeline.

**Resources Used:** Digital whiteboard for collaborative mapping, step-by-step guide handout

**Differentiation:** Provide more structured guidance for novice groups, allow more autonomy for experienced strategists

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative mapping tool for group work and real-time sharing

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Deliver a detailed presentation on each step of the Wardley Mapping implementation process, addressing questions and clarifying concepts.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and relate the information to their draft implementation timelines.

**Resources Used:** Detailed PowerPoint presentation, Q&A session

**Differentiation:** Offer advanced concepts for experienced learners, provide simplified explanations for novices

**Technology Integration:** Use a live Q&A platform for real-time question submission and upvoting

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 75 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop where learners apply the seven steps to their own startup contexts. Provide guidance and feedback.

**Learner Activities:** Develop a customized action plan for implementing Wardley Mapping in their startup, including timelines, resource allocation, and potential challenges.

**Resources Used:** Action plan template, peer review rubric

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one coaching for those struggling, provide additional challenges for advanced learners

**Technology Integration:** Use project management software to create digital action plans

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a peer review process of their action plans. Conduct a final reflection and Q&A session.

**Learner Activities:** Present their action plans to peers, provide and receive feedback. Reflect on the learning process and potential impact on their startups.

**Resources Used:** Peer review forms, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Adjust presentation format based on learner preferences (e.g., visual, verbal, written)

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital feedback tool for structured peer reviews

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during group activities and discussions
  - Alignment: Assesses understanding and application of concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of the final action plan for Wardley Mapping implementation
  - Alignment: Demonstrates ability to apply learning to specific startup contexts
* **Summative**: Peer review of action plans
  - Alignment: Encourages critical thinking and evaluation skills relevant to professional contexts

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide additional scaffolding, simplified examples, and more structured guidance
* **Experienced executives**: Offer advanced case studies, encourage mentoring of less experienced peers, and provide opportunities for deeper analysis
* **Technical vs. non-technical backgrounds**: Adjust examples and language to resonate with different professional backgrounds
