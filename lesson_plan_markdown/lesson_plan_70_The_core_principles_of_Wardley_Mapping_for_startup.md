# Mastering Wardley Mapping for Startup Success

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, entrepreneurs, and business strategists

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a startup's value chain using Wardley Mapping principles | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the evolutionary stages of business components in a startup context | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map for a startup scenario, incorporating all core principles | Creating |
| Synthesize strategic decisions for a startup based on insights from Wardley Mapping | Synthesizing |

## Key Concepts
* Value Chain Visualisation
* Component Evolution
* Situational Awareness
* Movement and Gameplay
* Lean Integration
* Iterative Mapping
* Ecosystem Thinking

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of value chains and business models

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital mapping tool
* Sticky notes or digital equivalents
* Case study materials of real startups
* Access to Wardley Mapping software or templates

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a startup scenario with complex strategic challenges. Facilitate a discussion on current approaches to strategic decision-making in startups.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze the given startup scenario, identify key challenges, and discuss potential strategic approaches in small groups.

**Resources Used:** Startup case study, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with startup environments

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative digital whiteboard for group brainstorming

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce the core principles of Wardley Mapping. Guide learners through a simple mapping exercise using a familiar business example.

**Learner Activities:** Create a basic Wardley Map for a well-known company, focusing on understanding the key components and their relationships.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping guide, sample maps

**Differentiation:** Offer simplified templates for novices and more complex scenarios for experienced strategists

**Technology Integration:** Utilize Wardley Mapping software for hands-on practice

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Deep dive into each core principle of Wardley Mapping for startups. Provide examples of how each principle applies in startup contexts.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in interactive lectures, ask questions, and discuss real-world applications of each principle.

**Resources Used:** Slide deck, video examples of successful Wardley Mapping in startups

**Differentiation:** Provide additional readings for those wanting to delve deeper into specific principles

**Technology Integration:** Use audience response systems for real-time comprehension checks

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a comprehensive Wardley Mapping exercise using a complex startup scenario. Provide guidance and feedback throughout the process.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small teams to create a detailed Wardley Map for the given startup scenario, applying all core principles. Present and defend strategic recommendations based on the map.

**Resources Used:** Detailed startup case study, Wardley Mapping templates

**Differentiation:** Assign roles within teams based on experience levels and strengths

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative mapping tools for team exercises

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Lead a critique session of the created maps and strategies. Facilitate peer feedback and self-reflection.

**Learner Activities:** Present team maps and strategies, provide constructive feedback to peers, reflect on personal learning and application to own startup or business.

**Resources Used:** Evaluation rubric, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Offer additional challenges for advanced learners, such as considering multiple future scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use digital portfolios for learners to document their work and reflections

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses application of core principles in real-time, mimicking the iterative nature of startup strategy
* **Summative**: Final presentation of Wardley Map and strategic recommendations for a startup scenario
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to synthesize learning into actionable business strategies

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice entrepreneurs**: Provide additional scaffolding in mapping exercises, offer simplified scenarios
* **Experienced business strategists**: Challenge with more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of ecosystem dynamics

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections

* Use Wardley Mapping to inform pivot decisions in startups

* Develop a 90-day action plan based on insights from the mapping exercise
* Form a peer group for ongoing Wardley Mapping practice and discussion

* Remind participants about confidentiality when discussing real business strategies
* Encourage a supportive environment for sharing and critiquing ideas
