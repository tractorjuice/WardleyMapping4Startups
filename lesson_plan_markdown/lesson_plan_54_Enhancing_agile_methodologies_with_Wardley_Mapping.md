# Enhancing Agile Methodologies with Wardley Mapping for Strategic Innovation

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in startup environments, particularly those working with government and public sector projects

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the integration of Wardley Mapping with agile methodologies to enhance strategic decision-making in startups | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of Wardley Mapping in addressing key challenges in agile development for public sector projects | Evaluating |
| Create a strategic backlog prioritization plan using Wardley Mapping principles | Creating |
| Design an enhanced sprint planning process that incorporates Wardley Mapping insights | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Agile methodologies
* Strategic backlog prioritization
* Enhanced sprint planning
* Stakeholder communication
* Long-term vision alignment
* Risk identification and mitigation
* Public sector innovation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of agile methodologies
* Familiarity with startup environments
* General knowledge of project management principles

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaboration tool
* Wardley Mapping templates
* Case study materials
* Access to online Wardley Mapping resources

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of the challenges faced by startups in the public sector. Introduce the concept of enhancing agile methodologies with Wardley Mapping.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion about their experiences with agile methodologies and strategic planning in startups.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide visual aids and written summaries for diverse learning styles

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather initial thoughts and experiences

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through the key components of Wardley Mapping and its potential integration with agile methodologies.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, explore sample Wardley Maps and discuss how they could enhance various aspects of agile development.

**Resources Used:** Sample Wardley Maps, guided exploration worksheets

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support and resources for those less familiar with strategic mapping concepts

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tools for group exploration

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed explanations of how Wardley Mapping enhances strategic backlog prioritization, sprint planning, stakeholder communication, and risk management.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and participate in guided discussions on each enhancement area.

**Resources Used:** Detailed presentation slides, real-world examples

**Differentiation:** Offer advanced concepts for experienced professionals, while providing foundational explanations for novices

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software with embedded quizzes for immediate feedback

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce the GovTech Innovator case study and facilitate a hands-on workshop on applying Wardley Mapping to agile processes.

**Learner Activities:** Work in teams to apply Wardley Mapping principles to a simulated startup scenario, creating strategic plans and enhanced agile processes.

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, Wardley Mapping templates, agile process worksheets

**Differentiation:** Assign roles within teams based on individual strengths and areas for growth

**Technology Integration:** Use project management and mapping software commonly used in professional settings

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide teams in presenting their strategic plans and facilitate peer evaluation and discussion.

**Learner Activities:** Present team solutions, provide peer feedback, and reflect on the application of Wardley Mapping to their own professional contexts.

**Resources Used:** Evaluation rubrics, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Offer various presentation formats to accommodate different communication preferences

**Technology Integration:** Use digital portfolios for teams to showcase their work and receive feedback

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during group activities and discussions
  - Alignment: Assesses understanding and application of concepts in real-time, mimicking professional feedback loops
* **Summative**: Team presentation of enhanced agile strategy using Wardley Mapping
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to synthesize and apply learned concepts to a realistic startup scenario
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on applying Wardley Mapping to learner's specific professional context
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to transfer learning to personal work environment and identify potential impacts

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice professionals**: Provide additional resources on basic Wardley Mapping concepts, offer more structured guidance during activities
* **Experienced professionals**: Offer advanced challenges, such as considering multiple stakeholder perspectives or complex regulatory environments
* **Visual learners**: Emphasize the use of Wardley Maps and other visual tools throughout the lesson
* **Kinesthetic learners**: Incorporate physical mapping exercises and role-playing activities
