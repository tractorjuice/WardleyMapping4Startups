# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Strategic Pivots in Startups

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, product managers, and strategy consultants in the tech industry

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a startup's market position using Wardley Mapping techniques | Analyzing |
| Evaluate potential pivot opportunities revealed through Wardley Mapping | Evaluating |
| Create a strategic pivot plan based on Wardley Mapping insights | Creating |
| Synthesize Wardley Mapping with Lean Startup methodologies for effective decision-making | Synthesizing |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Strategic Pivots
* Market Evolution
* Value Chain Analysis
* Predictive Analytics
* Lean Startup Methodology
* Govtech Sector

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of startup operations
* Familiarity with business strategy concepts
* General knowledge of technology trends

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or large sheets of paper and markers
* Case study materials (Startup X)
* Whiteboard and markers for group discussions

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of Startup X's initial challenges. Pose thought-provoking questions about market positioning and pivot decisions.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a think-pair-share activity to discuss potential strategies for Startup X based on initial information.

**Resources Used:** Startup X case study introduction

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with the govtech sector

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital polling tool to gather initial thoughts and display results in real-time

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through the process of creating a basic Wardley Map for Startup X's initial position. Introduce key Wardley Mapping concepts.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create a Wardley Map for Startup X using provided software or materials. Identify key components and their evolution.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software or mapping materials, Wardley Mapping guide

**Differentiation:** Provide templates or starting points for those new to Wardley Mapping

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tools for remote or hybrid sessions

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present the full case study of Startup X, highlighting how Wardley Mapping revealed new opportunities. Explain the connection between mapping insights and strategic decisions.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze the provided Wardley Map for Startup X, comparing it to their own maps. Discuss the implications of the revealed insights.

**Resources Used:** Detailed Startup X case study, expert-created Wardley Map

**Differentiation:** Provide additional explanations or resources for complex concepts

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software to annotate and highlight key aspects of the Wardley Map

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a discussion on how Wardley Mapping insights can be integrated with Lean Startup methodologies. Present additional scenarios for application.

**Learner Activities:** In groups, develop a pivot strategy for a fictional startup using Wardley Mapping insights. Create hypotheses and experiment plans based on the Lean Startup approach.

**Resources Used:** Lean Startup methodology overview, fictional startup scenarios

**Differentiation:** Offer varying levels of complexity in the fictional scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual collaboration tools for group work and presentation

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a reflection process on the application of Wardley Mapping in their own professional contexts. Facilitate peer feedback on pivot strategies.

**Learner Activities:** Present group pivot strategies. Individually, outline how Wardley Mapping could be applied to their own startup or organization.

**Resources Used:** Evaluation rubric for pivot strategies

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written)

**Technology Integration:** Use online forms for self-assessment and peer feedback

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and Wardley Mapping process
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to analyze market positions using Wardley Mapping
* **Formative**: Peer feedback on pivot strategies
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to create and communicate strategic plans based on Wardley Mapping insights
* **Summative**: Individual application plan for using Wardley Mapping in own context
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to synthesize learning and apply it to real-world professional situations

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice Wardley Mappers**: Provide simplified templates, additional guidance, and basic scenarios
* **Experienced strategists**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of market evolution
* **Non-tech sector professionals**: Provide additional context for tech trends, focus on universal strategic principles
