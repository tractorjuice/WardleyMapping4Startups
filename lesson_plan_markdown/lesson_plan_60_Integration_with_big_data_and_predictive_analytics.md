# Leveraging Big Data and Predictive Analytics in Wardley Mapping for Strategic Decision-Making

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in startup management, business strategy, and data analytics roles

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the integration of big data and predictive analytics with Wardley Mapping for enhanced strategic decision-making | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the potential impact of data-driven Wardley Mapping on startup strategy and competitive positioning | Evaluating |
| Create a data-enhanced Wardley Map for a given startup scenario | Creating |
| Propose strategies to overcome challenges in implementing data-driven Wardley Mapping | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Big Data Analytics
* Predictive Analytics
* Strategic Decision-Making
* Component Positioning
* Dynamic Evolution Tracking
* Predictive Scenario Modeling
* Real-time Market Intelligence
* Personalized Value Chain Optimization

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of Wardley Mapping principles
* Familiarity with data analytics concepts
* Experience in startup or business strategy roles

## Materials Needed
* Computers with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or online tool
* Sample datasets for analysis
* Case study materials
* Whiteboard or digital collaboration tool

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world startup scenario facing strategic challenges. Introduce the concept of integrating big data and predictive analytics with Wardley Mapping.

**Learner Activities:** Discuss in small groups how traditional Wardley Mapping might address the scenario and brainstorm potential benefits of data integration.

**Resources Used:** Scenario description, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with Wardley Mapping or data analytics

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital whiteboard for collaborative brainstorming

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through an exploration of key concepts: enhanced component positioning, dynamic evolution tracking, predictive scenario modeling, real-time market intelligence, and personalized value chain optimization.

**Learner Activities:** In pairs, research and analyze one assigned concept, preparing a brief presentation for the class.

**Resources Used:** Online resources, academic papers, industry reports

**Differentiation:** Provide concept summaries for those needing additional support

**Technology Integration:** Use of online research tools and collaborative presentation software

### Explain
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate peer teaching sessions where pairs present their assigned concepts. Provide additional explanations and real-world examples to reinforce understanding.

**Learner Activities:** Present findings to the class, engage in Q&A, and take notes on other presentations.

**Resources Used:** Learner-created presentations, facilitator-prepared examples

**Differentiation:** Encourage advanced learners to provide more in-depth analysis

**Technology Integration:** Use of presentation software and interactive Q&A tools

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce a complex case study of a startup integrating big data and predictive analytics with Wardley Mapping. Guide learners through the process of creating a data-enhanced Wardley Map.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to analyze the case study, create a data-enhanced Wardley Map, and develop strategic recommendations.

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, Wardley Mapping software, sample datasets

**Differentiation:** Provide additional guidance or challenges based on group progress

**Technology Integration:** Use of Wardley Mapping software and data visualization tools

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations of their data-enhanced Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations. Lead a discussion on the challenges and opportunities of this approach.

**Learner Activities:** Present group findings, provide peer feedback, and reflect on the learning experience.

**Resources Used:** Group-created Wardley Maps and presentations

**Differentiation:** Encourage advanced groups to consider more complex scenarios or additional data sources

**Technology Integration:** Use of presentation software and online feedback tools

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and peer teaching sessions
  - Alignment: Assesses understanding of key concepts and ability to explain them to peers
* **Summative**: Evaluation of group-created data-enhanced Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply learned concepts to a real-world scenario and create actionable insights
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on the potential impact and challenges of data-driven Wardley Mapping in their professional context
  - Alignment: Evaluates critical thinking and ability to connect learning to personal professional experiences

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice Wardley Mappers**: Provide additional resources on Wardley Mapping basics, pair with more experienced learners
* **Data Analytics Experts**: Offer more advanced datasets and encourage exploration of complex predictive models
* **Experienced Strategists**: Challenge to consider multi-industry scenarios or long-term strategic implications
