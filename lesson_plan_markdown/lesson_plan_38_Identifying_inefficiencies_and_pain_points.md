# Identifying Inefficiencies and Pain Points in Value Chains: A Wardley Mapping Approach

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in government, public sector, and startup environments

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a complex value chain using Wardley Mapping techniques to identify inefficiencies and pain points | Analyze |
| Evaluate user feedback and market trends to uncover opportunities for innovation in public sector services | Evaluate |
| Create a strategy to address identified inefficiencies and pain points in a government or public sector context | Create |
| Synthesize insights from value chain analysis, user feedback, and regulatory considerations to propose innovative solutions | Synthesize |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Value Chain Analysis
* Inefficiencies and Pain Points
* User-Centric Research
* Technological Evolution
* Regulatory Impact
* Disruptive Innovation
* Public Sector Innovation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with public sector or government operations
* Basic knowledge of user research methods

## Materials Needed
* Computers with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or large sheets of paper and markers
* Case study materials of a public sector service
* User feedback data sets
* Regulatory framework documents

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world scenario of a problematic public service. Guide a discussion on potential inefficiencies and their impacts.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in group discussion, brainstorm potential issues based on professional experiences

**Resources Used:** Slide presentation of case study, whiteboard for capturing ideas

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with public sector operations

**Technology Integration:** Use digital whiteboard for remote participants to contribute ideas

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce Wardley Mapping technique. Guide learners through the process of mapping the value chain for the case study.

**Learner Activities:** Create Wardley Maps in small groups, focusing on different aspects of the value chain

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software or paper templates, case study materials

**Differentiation:** Provide pre-made partial maps for less experienced learners

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative mapping software for real-time group work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Lead a discussion on identifying inefficiencies and pain points from the maps. Introduce concepts of user feedback analysis and technological evolution.

**Learner Activities:** Present group findings, discuss patterns and insights across different maps

**Resources Used:** Completed Wardley Maps, user feedback data sets

**Differentiation:** Offer guided analysis questions for groups needing more support

**Technology Integration:** Use data visualization tools to present user feedback trends

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce regulatory considerations. Guide learners in synthesizing insights to propose innovative solutions.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze regulatory documents, brainstorm solutions that address inefficiencies while complying with regulations

**Resources Used:** Regulatory framework documents, innovation case studies

**Differentiation:** Provide simplified regulatory summaries for those less familiar with legal language

**Technology Integration:** Use mind-mapping software to connect insights and generate ideas

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations of proposed solutions. Lead a discussion on the feasibility and potential impact of each solution.

**Learner Activities:** Present group solutions, provide peer feedback, reflect on the application of concepts to own professional context

**Resources Used:** Presentation software, evaluation rubrics

**Differentiation:** Offer presentation templates for less experienced presenters

**Technology Integration:** Use online polling for real-time peer feedback on presentations

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and Wardley Mapping process
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to analyze complex systems and identify inefficiencies
* **Summative**: Group presentation of innovative solutions
  - Alignment: Evaluates synthesis of insights and creation of strategies to address inefficiencies
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on applying concepts to own professional context
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to transfer learning to real-world scenarios

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice professionals**: Provide additional guidance on Wardley Mapping techniques, offer simplified versions of regulatory documents
* **Experienced professionals**: Encourage deeper analysis of systemic issues, challenge to consider cross-sector implications
