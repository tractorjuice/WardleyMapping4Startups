# Navigating the Evolution Axis: From Genesis to Commodity in Wardley Mapping

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, product managers, and business strategists

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the four stages of the evolution axis in Wardley Mapping | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the strategic implications of component evolution for startups | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map incorporating the evolution axis for a startup scenario | Creating |
| Formulate strategies for innovation and resource allocation based on component evolution | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Evolution axis
* Genesis stage
* Custom-built stage
* Product (Rental) stage
* Commodity stage
* Innovation opportunities
* Competitive positioning
* Resource allocation
* Future planning
* Partnership strategies

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of technology trends

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaboration tool
* Wardley Mapping software or templates
* Case study materials
* Handouts on evolution axis stages

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world startup scenario facing strategic challenges. Introduce the concept of the evolution axis in Wardley Mapping.

**Learner Activities:** Brainstorm potential solutions to the startup scenario and discuss how understanding component evolution might help.

**Resources Used:** Startup scenario description, introductory video on Wardley Mapping

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with startup environments

**Technology Integration:** Use an interactive polling tool to gather initial thoughts on the scenario

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through an exploration of the four stages of the evolution axis using examples from various industries.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, analyze provided examples of components at different evolution stages. Identify characteristics of each stage.

**Resources Used:** Handouts with examples, digital resources on evolution stages

**Differentiation:** Provide more complex examples for experienced professionals, simpler ones for novices

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative mind-mapping tool to visualize the characteristics of each stage

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Deliver a mini-lecture on the strategic implications of the evolution axis for startups. Facilitate a discussion on real-world applications.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in discussion, share experiences related to component evolution in their own industries

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, case studies from successful startups

**Differentiation:** Encourage learners to relate content to their specific industry or role

**Technology Integration:** Use a virtual whiteboard for collaborative note-taking during the discussion

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce a complex startup scenario. Guide learners through the process of creating a Wardley Map incorporating the evolution axis.

**Learner Activities:** Work in teams to create a Wardley Map for the given scenario, focusing on component evolution and strategic implications

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software or templates, scenario description

**Differentiation:** Provide additional guidance or challenges based on learners' mapping experience

**Technology Integration:** Use Wardley Mapping software for collaborative map creation

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate team presentations of their Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations

**Learner Activities:** Present Wardley Maps, explain strategic decisions based on component evolution, provide peer feedback

**Resources Used:** Presentation tools, peer evaluation rubrics

**Differentiation:** Adjust presentation expectations based on learners' experience levels

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital feedback tool for real-time peer evaluations

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and mapping activities
  - Alignment: Assesses understanding of evolution axis concepts and application to real scenarios
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create and use Wardley Maps for strategic decision-making

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice startup founders**: Provide additional resources on basic business strategy, offer more structured guidance in mapping activities
* **Experienced business strategists**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of cross-industry evolution patterns
