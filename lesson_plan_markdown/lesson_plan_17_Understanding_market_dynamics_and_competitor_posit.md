# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Market Dynamics and Competitor Positioning

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, product managers, and business strategists

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze market dynamics using Wardley Mapping techniques | Analyzing |
| Evaluate competitor positioning within a given market landscape | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map to visualize value chain and component evolution | Creating |
| Predict future market movements based on component evolution | Evaluating |
| Formulate strategic decisions based on Wardley Map insights | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Value Chain
* Component Evolution
* Market Dynamics
* Competitor Positioning
* Strategic Decision Making
* Innovation Opportunities

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of market analysis techniques

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or online tool
* Whiteboard and markers for group discussions
* Handouts with case studies and examples

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world startup scenario highlighting market challenges. Introduce Wardley Mapping as a solution to these challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion about current methods of analyzing market dynamics and their limitations.

**Resources Used:** PowerPoint presentation, video clip of a startup founder discussing market challenges

**Differentiation:** Provide multiple examples from different industries to cater to diverse professional backgrounds

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather learners' initial thoughts on market analysis techniques

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through the basic principles of Wardley Mapping. Demonstrate the creation of a simple Wardley Map.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create a basic Wardley Map for a given startup scenario. Share and discuss findings with the larger group.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, handouts with step-by-step instructions

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support for those new to mapping concepts, offer advanced scenarios for experienced strategists

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tools for group work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Elaborate on the key components of Wardley Mapping for market dynamics: identifying current market state, analyzing competitor positions, anticipating future movements, and uncovering opportunities.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze a pre-prepared Wardley Map, identifying key insights about market dynamics and competitor positioning. Participate in a facilitated discussion about these insights.

**Resources Used:** Detailed Wardley Map examples, analysis framework handout

**Differentiation:** Offer different levels of complexity in the maps provided for analysis

**Technology Integration:** Use digital annotation tools to highlight key areas of the maps during discussion

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 75 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present the CivicConnect case study. Guide learners through applying Wardley Mapping principles to a complex, real-world scenario.

**Learner Activities:** In groups, create a comprehensive Wardley Map for their own startup idea or a provided scenario. Use the map to develop strategic recommendations.

**Resources Used:** CivicConnect case study materials, Wardley Mapping software

**Differentiation:** Allow learners to choose between working on their own startup idea or a provided scenario

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual breakout rooms for group work, shared digital workspaces for collaborative mapping

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations of Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations. Provide feedback and lead a reflective discussion on the learning process.

**Learner Activities:** Present group Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations. Participate in peer evaluation and reflective discussion.

**Resources Used:** Rubric for evaluating Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations

**Differentiation:** Offer various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written) to accommodate different communication preferences

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing for presentations, digital feedback tools for peer evaluation

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Map and strategic recommendations
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create a comprehensive map and derive strategic insights
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on applying Wardley Mapping to their professional context
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to transfer learning to specific professional situations

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide additional guidance on mapping techniques, offer simplified scenarios for initial practice
* **Experienced business professionals**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of market dynamics and strategic implications
* **Industry-specific professionals**: Provide industry-specific examples and scenarios, encourage application to their specific market contexts
