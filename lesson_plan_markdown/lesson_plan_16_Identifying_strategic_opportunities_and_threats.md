# Leveraging Wardley Mapping to Identify Strategic Opportunities and Threats for Startups

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, executives, and strategic planners in technology-driven industries

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a startup's value chain using Wardley Mapping to identify strategic positioning and potential areas for growth | Analyze |
| Evaluate component evolution within a Wardley Map to anticipate future trends and innovation opportunities | Evaluate |
| Create a Wardley Map to visualize competitor positioning and identify market opportunities | Create |
| Synthesize insights from Wardley Maps to formulate strategies for leveraging network effects and building ecosystems | Synthesize |
| Predict potential industry disruptions and regulatory changes using Wardley Mapping techniques | Evaluate |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Value Chain Analysis
* Component Evolution
* Strategic Positioning
* Market Disruption
* Network Effects
* Ecosystem Development
* Regulatory Anticipation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of technology trends

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or large format paper and markers
* Case study materials
* Handouts on Wardley Mapping principles

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world scenario of a startup facing strategic challenges. Introduce Wardley Mapping as a tool for strategic analysis.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion on current methods of identifying opportunities and threats in their startups.

**Resources Used:** Slide presentation, whiteboard for brainstorming

**Differentiation:** Use varied examples from different industries to engage diverse participants

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather initial thoughts on strategic analysis methods

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the basics of creating a Wardley Map, focusing on value chain and evolution axis.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create a basic Wardley Map for a fictional startup, identifying key components and their positions.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software or paper templates

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support for those new to mapping, allow advanced participants to add more complexity

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tools for remote or hybrid sessions

### Explain
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed explanations of how to interpret Wardley Maps for strategic insights, using examples from the content.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze sample Wardley Maps to identify opportunities and threats, sharing insights with the larger group.

**Resources Used:** Pre-prepared Wardley Maps of real companies, analysis worksheet

**Differentiation:** Provide maps of varying complexity to suit different experience levels

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing and annotation tools to highlight key areas of maps during discussion

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop where participants apply Wardley Mapping to their own startups or case studies.

**Learner Activities:** Create detailed Wardley Maps for their startups, identify strategic opportunities and threats, and develop action plans.

**Resources Used:** Mapping software, action plan templates

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one coaching for those needing additional support

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms for small group collaboration in virtual settings

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants in presenting their Wardley Maps and strategic insights to the group for peer feedback.

**Learner Activities:** Present findings, provide constructive feedback to peers, and refine their strategic plans based on feedback.

**Resources Used:** Presentation software, peer evaluation rubrics

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., formal presentations, gallery walks)

**Technology Integration:** Use digital collaboration boards for sharing and commenting on maps

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Maps and strategic plans
  - Alignment: Measures ability to synthesize insights and create actionable strategies
* **Peer Assessment**: Structured feedback on presented strategies
  - Alignment: Encourages critical thinking and collaborative learning

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide simplified templates, additional guidance, and focus on basic concepts
* **Experienced strategists**: Offer advanced case studies, encourage deeper analysis of ecosystem dynamics
* **Industry-specific groups**: Tailor examples and discussions to relevant sectors (e.g., SaaS, hardware, biotech)
