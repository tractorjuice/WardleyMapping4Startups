# Spotting Opportunities for Vertical and Horizontal Integration in Public Sector Startups

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in public sector startups and government innovation roles

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a public sector startup's value chain using Wardley Mapping techniques | Analyzing |
| Evaluate potential opportunities for vertical and horizontal integration within a given public sector context | Evaluating |
| Design an integration strategy that aligns with broader policy objectives and citizen needs | Creating |
| Critique integration decisions based on their potential impact on service quality, efficiency, and regulatory compliance | Evaluating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Vertical Integration
* Horizontal Integration
* Value Chain Analysis
* Public Sector Innovation
* Strategic Decision-Making
* Competitive Advantage
* Citizen-Centric Services

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of startup operations
* Familiarity with public sector challenges and objectives
* General knowledge of business strategy concepts

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or large sheets of paper and markers
* Case study materials of public sector startups
* Whiteboard and markers for group discussions

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world scenario of a public sector startup facing integration challenges. Facilitate a brief discussion on potential solutions.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in group discussion, brainstorm initial ideas for addressing the startup's challenges.

**Resources Used:** Prepared case study, whiteboard for capturing ideas

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with public sector operations

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital whiteboard for remote participants to contribute ideas

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through the process of creating a Wardley Map for the case study startup. Introduce key concepts of vertical and horizontal integration.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to create Wardley Maps, identify potential integration opportunities

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software or paper templates, integration concept guide

**Differentiation:** Provide pre-made partial maps for groups needing additional support

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative mapping software for real-time group work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed explanations of vertical and horizontal integration strategies in the public sector context. Showcase examples from successful startups.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and relate concepts to their own professional experiences

**Resources Used:** Slide presentation, video case studies

**Differentiation:** Provide supplementary reading materials for advanced learners

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software with embedded quizzes

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a hands-on workshop where learners apply integration strategies to their Wardley Maps. Provide guidance and feedback.

**Learner Activities:** Revise Wardley Maps, develop integration proposals, peer review strategies

**Resources Used:** Integration strategy worksheet, peer review rubric

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one consultations for learners needing additional support

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms for virtual peer reviews and discussions

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Administer a summative assessment quiz and facilitate a final reflection discussion

**Learner Activities:** Complete assessment, participate in reflection discussion, set personal action items

**Resources Used:** Online quiz platform, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide extended time for quiz completion if needed

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital survey tool for immediate assessment feedback

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Peer review of integration strategies during the Elaborate phase
  - Alignment: Aligns with objectives on evaluating integration opportunities and designing strategies
* **Summative**: Quiz on key concepts and application of integration strategies in public sector contexts
  - Alignment: Covers all learning objectives, with emphasis on analysis and evaluation skills

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice professionals in public sector innovation**: Provide additional background materials on public sector operations and challenges
* **Experienced startup founders**: Offer advanced case studies and opportunities to mentor less experienced participants

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How can the integration strategies discussed today be applied in your current role?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing these strategies in your organization?
* How has your understanding of value chain analysis in the public sector evolved?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did the Wardley Mapping activity engage learners of different experience levels?
* What aspects of integration strategy were most challenging for learners to grasp?
* How can the real-world applications be more closely tailored to the specific roles of participants?
