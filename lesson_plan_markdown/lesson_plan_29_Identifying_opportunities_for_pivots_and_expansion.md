# Identifying Opportunities for Pivots and Expansions Using Wardley Mapping

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, product managers, and business strategists

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a startup's current market position using Wardley Mapping | Analyzing |
| Evaluate potential pivot and expansion opportunities based on Wardley Map insights | Evaluating |
| Create a strategic plan for a pivot or expansion using Wardley Mapping and Lean Startup principles | Creating |
| Integrate Wardley Mapping into existing customer development processes | Applying |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Pivot
* Expansion
* Value Chain
* Component Evolution
* Market Gaps
* Customer Development
* Lean Startup Methodology

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup concepts
* General knowledge of market analysis techniques

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or large sheets of paper and markers
* Case study materials
* Handouts on Wardley Mapping basics

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world case study of a successful pivot or expansion. Facilitate a discussion on how the decision was made and what information was used.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze the case study in small groups, discussing potential factors that led to the pivot or expansion decision.

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with the industry in the case study

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative digital whiteboard for group brainstorming and sharing ideas

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce Wardley Mapping concepts and guide learners through creating a basic map for a hypothetical startup.

**Learner Activities:** Create individual Wardley Maps for the hypothetical startup, identifying key components and their evolution.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software or paper/markers, step-by-step guide

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one assistance for those struggling with the mapping concept

**Technology Integration:** Use Wardley Mapping software with built-in tutorials and examples

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed information on using Wardley Maps to identify pivot and expansion opportunities. Provide examples and best practices.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and participate in discussions about applying Wardley Mapping to their own contexts.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, real-world examples

**Differentiation:** Provide supplementary reading materials for those wanting deeper insights

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software with embedded quizzes and polls

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a hands-on exercise of creating a Wardley Map for their own startup or a provided case study, focusing on identifying pivot or expansion opportunities.

**Learner Activities:** Create detailed Wardley Maps, analyze them for opportunities, and develop strategic recommendations based on the insights gained.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, worksheets for opportunity analysis

**Differentiation:** Pair experienced mappers with novices for peer support

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms in video conferencing software for small group collaboration

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations of Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations. Provide feedback and lead a reflective discussion.

**Learner Activities:** Present their Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations to the group, justify their decisions, and provide peer feedback.

**Resources Used:** Presentation equipment, evaluation rubric

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written)

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing for presentations and collaborative feedback tools for peer evaluation

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during the Explore and Elaborate phases
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create and analyze Wardley Maps in real-time
* **Summative**: Final presentation of Wardley Map and strategic recommendations
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to synthesize learning and apply it to a real-world scenario
* **Summative**: Written reflection on how they plan to integrate Wardley Mapping into their customer development process
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to connect learning to professional practice

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice professionals**: Provide additional scaffolding, such as partially completed maps or guided exercises
* **Experienced strategists**: Offer more complex scenarios and encourage deeper analysis of market dynamics
* **Visual learners**: Emphasize the visual aspects of Wardley Mapping and provide additional diagramming tools
* **Verbal learners**: Incorporate more discussion-based activities and written analysis components

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Product management: Using Wardley Maps for product roadmapping
* Marketing: Identifying new market segments through value chain analysis
* Finance: Assessing potential ROI of pivot or expansion opportunities
* Technology: Understanding the evolution of technological components in the value chain

## Real-World Applications
* Identifying underserved markets or customer segments
* Anticipating industry disruptions and positioning the company accordingly
* Optimizing resource allocation for maximum competitive advantage
* Improving strategic decision-making processes within the organization

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflection on personal biases in strategic decision-making
* Analysis of how Wardley Mapping changes their perspective on their business
* Consideration of how this approach compares to their current strategic planning processes

## Extension Activities
* Create a Wardley Map for a competitor and compare it to your own
* Develop a 12-month action plan based on the opportunities identified in the Wardley Map
* Conduct customer interviews to validate assumptions made in the Wardley Map

## Safety Considerations
* Remind participants to respect confidentiality when discussing real business strategies
* Encourage ethical considerations when identifying market opportunities

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How does Wardley Mapping change your understanding of your business's strategic position?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing Wardley Mapping in your organization?
* How might Wardley Mapping complement or replace your current strategic planning tools?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did learners grasp the concept of Wardley Mapping?
* What aspects of the lesson seemed to resonate most with the participants?
* How can the lesson be improved to better address the diverse needs of professional learners?
