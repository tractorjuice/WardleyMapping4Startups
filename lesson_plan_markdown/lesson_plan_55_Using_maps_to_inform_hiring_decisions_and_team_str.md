# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Strategic Hiring and Team Structure in Startups

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, HR professionals, and team leaders in the government and public sector technology space

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a startup's value chain using Wardley Mapping to identify skill gaps and prioritize hiring needs | Analyzing |
| Design team structures that align with the evolutionary stages of components in a Wardley Map | Creating |
| Evaluate future hiring needs based on the anticipated evolution of components in a Wardley Map | Evaluating |
| Apply Wardley Mapping principles to make informed decisions about resource allocation in a startup context | Applying |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Value chain analysis
* Component evolution
* Strategic hiring
* Team structure optimization
* Resource allocation
* Skill gap analysis
* Future needs anticipation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of startup operations
* Familiarity with general HR practices
* Basic knowledge of strategic planning concepts

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaborative space
* Wardley Mapping templates
* Case study materials
* Laptops or tablets for participants
* Access to online Wardley Mapping tools

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of a fictional startup facing hiring and team structure challenges. Introduce the concept of Wardley Mapping and its potential to address these challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion about current hiring and team structure challenges in their own organizations.

**Resources Used:** PowerPoint presentation, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide visual aids and written summaries for diverse learning styles

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather and display participant responses

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the process of creating a basic Wardley Map for the fictional startup. Demonstrate how to identify components and their evolutionary stages.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to create Wardley Maps for their own organizations or the provided case study.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping templates, online mapping tools

**Differentiation:** Provide step-by-step guides for novices and advanced challenges for experienced mappers

**Technology Integration:** Utilize online collaborative mapping tools for group work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed explanations of how to use Wardley Maps for identifying skill gaps, prioritizing hires, and structuring teams. Use the CivicInnovate case study as an example.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze the CivicInnovate case study, identifying key decisions made based on their Wardley Map.

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, presentation slides

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context and examples for those less familiar with GovTech

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive case study software for enhanced engagement

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop where participants apply Wardley Mapping to their own organizations' hiring and team structure challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Create action plans for strategic hiring and team restructuring based on their Wardley Maps.

**Resources Used:** Action plan templates, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one coaching for participants who need additional support

**Technology Integration:** Use project management software to create and share action plans

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through a peer review process of their action plans. Facilitate a whole-group discussion on key learnings and potential implementation challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Present action plans to peers, provide and receive feedback, reflect on the application of Wardley Mapping to their professional context.

**Resources Used:** Evaluation rubrics, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Allow for written or verbal presentations based on participant preference

**Technology Integration:** Use digital feedback tools for anonymous peer reviews

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses participants' ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final action plans for strategic hiring and team restructuring
  - Alignment: Measures participants' ability to synthesize learning and apply it to their professional context

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice Wardley Mappers**: Provide additional guidance, simplified templates, and basic examples
* **Experienced strategists**: Offer advanced scenarios, encourage deeper analysis, and facilitate peer teaching opportunities
