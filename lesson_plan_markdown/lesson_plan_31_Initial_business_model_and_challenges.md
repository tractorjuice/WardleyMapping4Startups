# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Business Model Innovation

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, business strategists, and innovation managers

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a startup's initial business model and challenges using Wardley Mapping | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of Wardley Mapping in identifying pivot opportunities | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map for a given business scenario | Creating |
| Propose strategic pivots based on insights from Wardley Mapping | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Business Model Innovation
* Pivot Strategies
* Value Chain Analysis
* Market Evolution
* Regulatory Challenges
* Technology Adoption
* Data Security
* Scalability

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business models
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of strategic planning

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaboration tool
* Wardley Mapping templates
* Case study materials
* Laptops or tablets for participants

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present the Startup X case study and introduce Wardley Mapping concept

**Learner Activities:** Discuss initial reactions to the case and brainstorm potential challenges

**Resources Used:** Case study handout, presentation slides

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with government tech

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling for initial thoughts on the case

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the basics of creating a Wardley Map

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, attempt to create an initial Wardley Map for Startup X

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping templates, collaborative digital tools

**Differentiation:** Provide pre-filled templates for novice mappers

**Technology Integration:** Use online Wardley Mapping tools for collaborative mapping

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a detailed analysis of Startup X's Wardley Map and its insights

**Learner Activities:** Compare their maps with the presented analysis, noting differences and insights

**Resources Used:** Expert-created Wardley Map for Startup X, analysis slides

**Differentiation:** Offer additional explanations for complex map components

**Technology Integration:** Use annotation tools to highlight key map features

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a discussion on potential pivot strategies based on the map

**Learner Activities:** Develop and present pivot proposals for Startup X

**Resources Used:** Pivot strategy framework, case study updates

**Differentiation:** Provide industry-specific pivot examples for different sectors

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual breakout rooms for group strategy sessions

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Assess learner understanding through a practical application exercise

**Learner Activities:** Apply Wardley Mapping to their own business scenarios or a provided case

**Resources Used:** Assessment rubric, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Offer choice in assessment format (written, presentation, or map creation)

**Technology Integration:** Use digital submission tools for assessments

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and map creation process
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Creation of a Wardley Map and pivot proposal for a given business scenario
  - Alignment: Evaluates comprehensive understanding and application of learned concepts

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Experienced strategists**: Provide more complex scenarios and encourage deeper analysis of market dynamics
* **Novice entrepreneurs**: Offer additional guidance on business model components and mapping basics

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Data analytics for market insights
* Legal considerations in pivoting strategies
* UX design in technology adoption challenges

## Real-World Applications
* Identifying new market opportunities in existing industries
* Navigating regulatory challenges in innovative sectors
* Optimizing resource allocation in startup environments

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflect on personal biases in strategic decision-making
* Analyze past business decisions through the lens of Wardley Mapping
* Identify gaps in current strategic thinking approaches

## Extension Activities
* Apply Wardley Mapping to participants' own business challenges
* Conduct a mini-research project on successful pivots in their industry
* Create a Wardley Map-based presentation for stakeholders

## Safety Considerations
* Ensure confidentiality when discussing personal business strategies
* Address potential emotional responses to pivoting and failure scenarios

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How might Wardley Mapping change your approach to strategic planning?
* What were the most challenging aspects of creating a Wardley Map?
* How can you integrate this tool into your current business practices?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did the case study illustrate key Wardley Mapping concepts?
* What areas of the mapping process seemed to challenge participants the most?
* How can the lesson be improved to better address diverse business backgrounds?

* Utilize asynchronous discussion forums for extended case analysis
