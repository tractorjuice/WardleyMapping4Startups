# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Startup Success

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, entrepreneurs, and early-stage startup team members

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the competitive landscape using Wardley Mapping techniques | Analyzing |
| Evaluate strategic opportunities for startups using Wardley Maps | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map for a startup scenario | Creating |
| Justify strategic decisions using insights from Wardley Mapping | Evaluating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Value Chain Visualization
* Evolutionary Stages
* Strategic Opportunities
* Rapid Iteration
* Resource Allocation
* Competitive Landscape Analysis

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of value chains and competitive analysis

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaborative platform
* Wardley Mapping templates
* Case study materials
* Laptops or tablets for participants

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a startup scenario with complex strategic challenges. Introduce Wardley Mapping as a potential solution.

**Learner Activities:** Discuss current approaches to strategic planning in their startups. Identify limitations of traditional methods.

**Resources Used:** Startup scenario description, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Adjust scenario complexity based on participants' experience levels

**Technology Integration:** Use online polling tool for gathering initial thoughts on strategic challenges

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the basic principles of Wardley Mapping. Demonstrate the creation of a simple Wardley Map.

**Learner Activities:** Practice creating basic Wardley Maps for hypothetical startup scenarios in small groups.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping tutorial video, practice scenarios

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support or advanced challenges based on group progress

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tool for group practice

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Elaborate on the key components of Wardley Mapping and their relevance to startups. Discuss real-world examples of successful application.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze sample Wardley Maps from successful startups. Identify key insights and strategic decisions derived from the maps.

**Resources Used:** Case studies of startups using Wardley Mapping, analysis worksheets

**Differentiation:** Offer additional context or complexity for more experienced participants

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software for dynamic case study exploration

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop where participants apply Wardley Mapping to their own startup scenarios or current strategic challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Create comprehensive Wardley Maps for their startups. Present and discuss strategic insights with peers.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping templates, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Provide individualized coaching based on each startup's unique challenges

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms in video conferencing for small group work and presentations

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide reflection on the Wardley Mapping process and its potential impact on participants' startups.

**Learner Activities:** Complete a self-assessment on their understanding and planned application of Wardley Mapping.

**Resources Used:** Self-assessment questionnaire, action plan template

**Differentiation:** Offer personalized feedback and resources based on self-assessment results

**Technology Integration:** Use online forms for self-assessment and feedback collection

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and practice mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Map created for participant's own startup
  - Alignment: Demonstrates ability to create and derive strategic insights from a Wardley Map
* **Summative**: Presentation of strategic insights derived from Wardley Mapping
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to communicate and justify strategic decisions using Wardley Mapping insights

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice entrepreneurs**: Provide additional guidance on basic business strategy concepts, offer simplified Wardley Mapping templates
* **Experienced founders**: Present more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of evolutionary stages and potential disruptions

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Product management: Using Wardley Maps for product roadmapping
* Marketing: Applying Wardley Mapping to understand customer journey and touchpoints
* Finance: Integrating Wardley Mapping insights into financial planning and resource allocation

## Real-World Applications
* Identifying potential areas for innovation or disruption in the market
* Making informed decisions about resource allocation and strategic investments
* Communicating startup strategy to investors and stakeholders
* Anticipating and preparing for market shifts and competitive threats

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflect on how Wardley Mapping challenges or enhances current strategic thinking processes
* Consider how the visual nature of Wardley Maps impacts understanding of complex business environments
* Analyze personal biases in strategic decision-making revealed through the mapping process

## Extension Activities
* Conduct a Wardley Mapping exercise with the entire startup team
* Create a series of Wardley Maps to track strategic evolution over time
* Use Wardley Mapping to inform the next major strategic decision or pivot

## Safety Considerations
* Ensure confidentiality of strategic information shared during group discussions
* Remind participants to focus on high-level strategy rather than proprietary details in shared exercises

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How does Wardley Mapping change your perspective on your startup's current strategy?
* What new opportunities or risks did you identify through the Wardley Mapping process?
* How might you integrate Wardley Mapping into your regular strategic planning processes?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did participants grasp and apply the Wardley Mapping concepts?
* What aspects of Wardley Mapping seemed most valuable or challenging for the participants?
* How can the workshop be improved to better meet the needs of startup founders and teams?

* Provide pre-recorded video tutorials for asynchronous learning of basic concepts
* Use online collaboration platforms for sharing and discussing Wardley Maps
