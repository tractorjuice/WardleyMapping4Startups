# Mastering Wardley Mapping for Strategic Innovation in Startups

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, entrepreneurs, and professionals in government and public sector innovation

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the key principles and components of Wardley Mapping in the context of startup strategy | Analyzing |
| Create a basic Wardley Map for a startup scenario, incorporating relevant elements of the business landscape | Creating |
| Evaluate the potential applications of Wardley Mapping in navigating uncertainty and driving innovation in startups | Evaluating |
| Synthesize Wardley Mapping techniques with other startup methodologies to develop comprehensive strategic plans | Synthesizing |

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world startup challenge and demonstrate how traditional strategy tools fall short. Introduce Wardley Mapping as a solution.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion on current strategic challenges in their startups or organizations.

**Resources Used:** Slide presentation, interactive polling tool

**Differentiation:** Provide options for participants to share experiences verbally or through written responses

**Technology Integration:** Use an online collaborative whiteboard for idea sharing

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the basic components of a Wardley Map using a simplified example.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, attempt to create a rudimentary Wardley Map for a given startup scenario.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping templates, scenario descriptions

**Differentiation:** Provide additional guidance to novice groups, challenge experienced participants with more complex scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Utilize online Wardley Mapping tools for digital creation and collaboration

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Deliver a comprehensive explanation of Wardley Mapping principles, techniques, and applications in startup contexts.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and relate concepts to their own experiences.

**Resources Used:** Detailed handouts, visual aids

**Differentiation:** Offer advanced reading materials for those with prior knowledge

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software with embedded quizzes

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 75 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop on applying Wardley Mapping to real startup challenges, including integration with other methodologies.

**Learner Activities:** Work on creating a detailed Wardley Map for their own startup or a provided case study, incorporating Lean Startup principles.

**Resources Used:** Case studies, Lean Startup integration guide

**Differentiation:** Provide one-on-one coaching for those struggling, offer additional challenges for quick learners

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms in virtual settings for small group work

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Lead a session where participants present their Wardley Maps and strategic insights.

**Learner Activities:** Present their maps, provide peer feedback, and reflect on the learning experience.

**Resources Used:** Evaluation rubric, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (verbal, visual, written)

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing for virtual presentations, digital feedback tools

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during group activities and map creation
  - Alignment: Assesses progress towards all learning objectives in a practical context
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Maps and strategic plans presented by participants
  - Alignment: Directly assesses the ability to create and apply Wardley Maps to real startup scenarios

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice entrepreneurs**: Provide additional scaffolding, simplified scenarios, and more direct guidance
* **Experienced startup founders**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis, and facilitate peer mentoring
* **Public sector professionals**: Focus on government-specific case studies and regulatory considerations
