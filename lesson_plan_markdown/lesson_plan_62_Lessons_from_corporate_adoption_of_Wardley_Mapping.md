# Leveraging Corporate Insights: Applying Wardley Mapping in Startups

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, executives, and strategic planners

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the key lessons from corporate adoption of Wardley Mapping | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the applicability of corporate Wardley Mapping insights to startup contexts | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map for a startup scenario, incorporating lessons from corporate adoption | Creating |
| Formulate strategies for implementing Wardley Mapping in a startup environment | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Cultural alignment
* Cross-functional collaboration
* Iterative mapping
* Strategic partnerships
* Stakeholder communication
* Skills development
* Integration with other methodologies
* Regulatory navigation
* Innovation identification

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of strategic planning
* Familiarity with startup operations and challenges
* Introductory knowledge of Wardley Mapping concepts

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaboration tool
* Wardley Mapping templates
* Case studies of corporate Wardley Mapping adoption
* Laptops or tablets for participants
* Projector or large screen for presentations

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of Wardley Mapping and its adoption in corporate settings. Pose thought-provoking questions about how this might apply to startups.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion on their current strategic planning methods and challenges.

**Resources Used:** Slide deck with corporate Wardley Mapping examples

**Differentiation:** Provide visual aids for visual learners and encourage verbal participation for auditory learners

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather initial thoughts and experiences

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through a detailed exploration of key lessons from corporate Wardley Mapping adoption. Facilitate small group discussions on each major point.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, analyze case studies of corporate Wardley Mapping implementation and discuss potential applications to startup scenarios.

**Resources Used:** Case study handouts, guided discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide both written and verbal instructions for activities. Offer additional support for groups as needed.

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online documents for group note-taking and idea sharing

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a comprehensive overview of how corporate Wardley Mapping insights can be adapted for startups. Address questions and facilitate class discussion.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and participate in discussions. Begin to formulate ideas for applying concepts to their own startups.

**Resources Used:** Detailed presentation slides, Q&A session

**Differentiation:** Provide handouts summarizing key points for review. Offer opportunities for both verbal and written questions.

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital Q&A platform to collect and address questions in real-time

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the process of creating a Wardley Map for their startup or a provided scenario. Provide feedback and support as needed.

**Learner Activities:** Create a Wardley Map incorporating lessons from corporate adoption. Present and discuss maps with peers.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping templates, scenario descriptions (if needed)

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one support for those struggling with the mapping process. Provide advanced challenges for those who finish quickly.

**Technology Integration:** Use digital mapping tools for creating and sharing Wardley Maps

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a group discussion on the created maps and strategies. Guide reflection on the learning process and potential implementation challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Present Wardley Maps to the group, provide peer feedback, and reflect on the application of corporate insights to their startup context.

**Resources Used:** Evaluation rubric, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (verbal, visual, written) to accommodate different communication styles.

**Technology Integration:** Use online feedback tools for peer evaluations and self-assessments

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during group discussions and mapping activities
  - Alignment: Assesses understanding and application of key concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Maps and implementation strategies
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create and apply Wardley Maps in a startup context
* **Peer Assessment**: Structured peer feedback on presented Wardley Maps
  - Alignment: Encourages critical thinking and collaborative learning

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice startup founders**: Provide additional scaffolding in Wardley Mapping techniques and startup strategy basics
* **Experienced entrepreneurs**: Offer more complex scenarios and encourage deeper analysis of industry-specific applications

* Remind participants to respect intellectual property rights when discussing strategies

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How does Wardley Mapping change your approach to startup strategy?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing Wardley Mapping in your startup?
* How can you adapt the corporate lessons to fit your unique startup context?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did participants grasp the connection between corporate insights and startup applications?
* What aspects of the lesson seemed most valuable to participants?
* How can the lesson be improved to better address the diverse needs of startup professionals?
