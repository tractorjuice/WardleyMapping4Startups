# Incorporating Wardley Mapping into Regular Strategy Reviews

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup executives, founders, and strategy teams

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the benefits of integrating Wardley Mapping into regular strategy reviews | Analyzing |
| Design a process for incorporating Wardley Mapping into a startup's strategic review cycle | Creating |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of Wardley Mapping in enhancing strategic decision-making | Evaluating |
| Apply Wardley Mapping techniques to a startup's current strategic challenges | Applying |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Strategic reviews
* Component evolution
* Competitive landscape analysis
* Action planning
* Scenario planning
* Innovation tracking

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of strategic planning processes
* Familiarity with startup operations and challenges
* Introductory knowledge of Wardley Mapping concepts

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital mapping tool
* Laptops or tablets for participants
* Case study materials
* Sample Wardley Maps
* Strategic review templates

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a case study of a startup that failed due to poor strategic planning. Facilitate a discussion on how Wardley Mapping could have potentially changed the outcome.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze the case study in small groups, identifying key strategic missteps and discussing how visual mapping might have helped.

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with startup challenges

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative digital whiteboard for group brainstorming

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the key aspects of incorporating Wardley Mapping into strategy reviews, using interactive demonstrations and examples.

**Learner Activities:** In pairs, practice creating simple Wardley Maps based on given scenarios, focusing on component positioning and evolution.

**Resources Used:** Sample Wardley Maps, mapping templates

**Differentiation:** Offer more complex scenarios for experienced participants

**Technology Integration:** Utilize a Wardley Mapping software tool for hands-on practice

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed explanations of advanced Wardley Mapping techniques such as scenario planning and competitive analysis. Demonstrate how these techniques enhance strategic reviews.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes and ask questions. Participate in a Q&A session to clarify understanding.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, example maps

**Differentiation:** Provide supplementary reading materials for those wanting deeper insights

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software with real-time polling

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 75 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop where participants apply Wardley Mapping to their own startup's strategic challenges. Provide guidance and feedback throughout the process.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small teams to create Wardley Maps for their startup, identify strategic insights, and develop action plans based on the maps.

**Resources Used:** Mapping templates, action plan worksheets

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one coaching for teams struggling with the mapping process

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative project management tools to document insights and action plans

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Lead a reflection session on the value of incorporating Wardley Mapping into strategy reviews. Facilitate peer feedback on the maps and action plans developed.

**Learner Activities:** Present team maps and action plans to the larger group. Provide constructive feedback to other teams. Reflect on personal learnings and potential applications.

**Resources Used:** Evaluation rubric, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Allow for written reflections for those less comfortable with verbal presentations

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital feedback tool for anonymous peer evaluations

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of participant engagement and understanding during mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Aligns with learning objectives related to applying Wardley Mapping techniques
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Maps and action plans created by teams
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to design and apply Wardley Mapping in strategic contexts
* **Summative**: Post-workshop assignment to implement Wardley Mapping in next strategic review and report on outcomes
  - Alignment: Evaluates long-term application and impact in professional setting

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide additional guidance on basic mapping techniques, offer simplified scenarios for practice
* **Experienced executives**: Challenge with complex, multi-factor scenarios, encourage mentoring of less experienced participants

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Product management: Using Wardley Maps for product roadmapping
* Marketing: Applying mapping to understand customer value chain
* Technology: Mapping technological dependencies and evolution

## Real-World Applications
* Conducting more effective quarterly strategy reviews
* Identifying new market opportunities based on component evolution
* Aligning investment decisions with strategic insights from maps

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflection on personal strategic thinking processes before and after learning Wardley Mapping
* Analysis of how mapping changes team dynamics in strategy discussions
* Journaling on the challenges and breakthroughs experienced while applying mapping to real scenarios

## Extension Activities
* Organize a company-wide Wardley Mapping workshop
* Create a library of Wardley Maps for key industry trends
* Develop a mentoring program for Wardley Mapping within the organization

## Safety Considerations
* Ensure confidentiality of strategic information shared during the workshop
* Discuss ethical considerations in competitive analysis and strategy formulation

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How has Wardley Mapping changed your approach to strategic thinking?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing Wardley Mapping in your regular strategy reviews?
* How might Wardley Mapping improve collaboration across different departments in your startup?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did participants grasp the connection between Wardley Mapping and strategic reviews?
* What aspects of the workshop seemed most valuable to participants?
* How can the workshop be improved to better address the diverse needs of startup professionals?
