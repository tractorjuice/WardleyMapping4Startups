# Keeping Wardley Maps Current: A Critical Practice for Startup Success

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, executives, and strategic planners

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the importance of regularly updating Wardley Maps in a startup context | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the consequences of relying on outdated Wardley Maps for strategic decision-making | Evaluating |
| Design a systematic approach for maintaining up-to-date Wardley Maps within a startup | Creating |
| Apply strategies to integrate Wardley Map updates into key business processes | Applying |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Strategic decision-making
* Business landscape evolution
* Living documents
* Map health checks
* Iterative mapping process

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of Wardley Mapping principles
* Familiarity with startup operations and challenges
* General strategic planning concepts

## Materials Needed
* Digital Wardley Mapping tool (e.g., Wardley Maps Online)
* Case study materials
* Collaborative online whiteboard (e.g., Miro)
* Access to relevant business metrics and market data

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief case study of a startup that failed due to outdated strategic mapping. Facilitate a discussion on potential causes and consequences.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze the case study in small groups, identify key issues, and share insights with the class.

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, collaborative online whiteboard

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with Wardley Mapping

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms for small group discussions, shared digital whiteboard for idea collection

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a self-assessment of their current Wardley Mapping practices. Introduce the concept of 'living maps' and facilitate exploration of various update strategies.

**Learner Activities:** Complete a self-assessment questionnaire, research different approaches to map maintenance, and discuss findings with peers.

**Resources Used:** Self-assessment tool, curated list of resources on map maintenance strategies

**Differentiation:** Provide more structured guidance for novices, allow experienced mappers to dive deeper into advanced techniques

**Technology Integration:** Use online survey tools for self-assessment, provide digital resource library

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present key principles and best practices for maintaining up-to-date Wardley Maps. Demonstrate the 'map health check' framework.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and participate in interactive demonstrations of map update techniques.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, live Wardley Mapping tool demonstration

**Differentiation:** Offer additional examples for complex concepts, provide summary handouts

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing for live demonstrations, interactive polling for concept checks

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a hands-on workshop where learners apply the map health check framework to a sample startup scenario.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to update a provided Wardley Map based on new information and changing market conditions.

**Resources Used:** Digital Wardley Mapping tool, scenario description and update prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide varying levels of complexity in scenarios, offer coaching support as needed

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative mapping tools, virtual breakout rooms for group work

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a reflection process on their updated maps and facilitate peer feedback sessions.

**Learner Activities:** Present updated maps to peers, provide and receive constructive feedback, reflect on the process and insights gained.

**Resources Used:** Peer evaluation rubric, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Adjust feedback expectations based on experience level, offer additional support for presentation skills if needed

**Technology Integration:** Use digital presentation tools, online feedback forms

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during group activities and discussions
  - Alignment: Assesses application of concepts and collaborative problem-solving skills
* **Summative**: Final presentation of updated Wardley Map and justification of changes
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to apply learned techniques and critically analyze strategic implications

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice Wardley Mappers**: Provide additional resources on basic mapping concepts, offer more structured guidance during activities
* **Experienced strategic planners**: Encourage exploration of advanced techniques, facilitate peer teaching opportunities

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Project management (integrating map updates with project milestones)
* Data analytics (using data to inform map updates)
* Organizational behavior (fostering a culture of continuous strategic reassessment)

## Real-World Applications
* Integrating Wardley Map updates into quarterly business reviews
* Using updated maps to guide product development priorities
* Leveraging map insights for investor presentations and funding rounds

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflection on personal biases in strategic thinking
* Analysis of how mapping practices have evolved over time in learners' organizations
* Identification of barriers to regular map updates and strategies to overcome them

## Extension Activities
* Develop a custom map health check framework for learners' specific organizations
* Create a plan for training team members on map maintenance
* Conduct a retrospective analysis of a past strategic decision using updated mapping techniques

## Safety Considerations
* Ensure confidentiality when discussing sensitive business information
* Address potential resistance to changing established strategic practices
