# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Strategic R&D Investment in Startups

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, executives, and R&D managers in technology-driven industries

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a startup's value chain using Wardley Mapping to identify critical components for R&D investment | Analyzing |
| Evaluate potential disruptive technologies and approaches using Wardley Mapping techniques | Evaluating |
| Create a strategic R&D investment plan that aligns with overall business strategy and market positioning | Creating |
| Synthesize insights from Wardley Mapping to balance short-term needs with long-term innovation goals | Synthesizing |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* R&D investment strategy
* Value chain analysis
* Technological evolution
* Disruptive innovation
* Strategic alignment
* Resource allocation
* Collaborative R&D
* Market positioning

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with R&D processes in startups
* General knowledge of technology trends in their industry

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or online tool
* Whiteboard and markers for group discussions
* Case study materials of tech startups
* Handouts on Wardley Mapping principles

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world scenario of a startup facing R&D investment decisions. Introduce Wardley Mapping as a strategic tool.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion on current challenges in R&D investment decision-making in their startups.

**Resources Used:** Slide presentation, interactive polling software

**Differentiation:** Use varied examples from different tech sectors to engage diverse participants

**Technology Integration:** Use an online collaborative whiteboard for brainstorming session

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the basics of creating a Wardley Map, focusing on value chain and evolution axis.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create a basic Wardley Map for a fictional startup, identifying key components and their evolutionary stage.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, step-by-step guide handout

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support for those new to Wardley Mapping, allow advanced users to explore more complex scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing to demonstrate Wardley Mapping software features

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present detailed explanations of how Wardley Mapping can guide R&D investment, using examples from the content provided.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze sample Wardley Maps to identify potential areas for R&D investment, discuss findings in pairs.

**Resources Used:** Case studies, sample Wardley Maps

**Differentiation:** Offer additional context for those less familiar with certain technologies or industries

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive Wardley Map visualizations to explain concepts

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a hands-on workshop where participants apply Wardley Mapping to their own startup scenarios.

**Learner Activities:** Create a comprehensive Wardley Map for their startup, identify strategic R&D priorities, and develop an investment plan.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, strategic planning templates

**Differentiation:** Provide one-on-one coaching for those struggling, offer advanced challenges for quick learners

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms in video conferencing for small group work and peer feedback

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants in presenting their Wardley Maps and R&D investment strategies, facilitate peer feedback and discussion.

**Learner Activities:** Present Wardley Maps and R&D strategies to peers, provide constructive feedback, reflect on learning and application to their startups.

**Resources Used:** Presentation software, peer evaluation rubrics

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written) to accommodate different communication styles

**Technology Integration:** Use online feedback tools for real-time peer evaluations

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during group activities and discussions
  - Alignment: Assesses participants' understanding and application of Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Map and R&D investment strategy presentation
  - Alignment: Assesses participants' ability to synthesize learning into a practical, strategic plan for their startup

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice Wardley Mappers**: Provide additional resources, simplified examples, and more guided practice
* **Experienced strategists**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of market dynamics and technological trends

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Connect to financial planning and budgeting processes
* Explore links with product management and development methodologies
* Discuss implications for human resources and talent acquisition strategies

## Real-World Applications
* Using Wardley Mapping to pitch to investors and justify R&D budgets
* Applying the technique to identify potential acquisition targets or partnership opportunities
* Leveraging maps to communicate strategic decisions to team members and stakeholders

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflect on how Wardley Mapping challenges or confirms existing assumptions about R&D investment
* Consider how this strategic approach might change decision-making processes in their startup
* Identify personal biases in technology assessment and how Wardley Mapping might mitigate them

## Extension Activities
* Create a series of time-horizon Wardley Maps (1, 3, 5 years) for long-term strategic planning
* Develop a Wardley Map-based presentation for board members or investors
* Collaborate with other participants to create an industry-wide Wardley Map

## Safety Considerations
* Remind participants about confidentiality when discussing their startup's strategies
* Encourage ethical considerations in R&D investment decisions

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How has Wardley Mapping changed your perspective on R&D investment in your startup?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing this approach, and how might you overcome them?
* How can you integrate Wardley Mapping into your regular strategic planning processes?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did participants engage with the Wardley Mapping concept?
* What aspects of the lesson seemed most valuable to participants?
* How can the lesson be improved to better meet the needs of diverse startup professionals?

## Adaptations for Virtual Learning
* Use breakout rooms for small group activities and discussions
* Leverage collaborative online Wardley Mapping tools for remote participation
* Incorporate more frequent check-ins and shorter activity durations to maintain engagement
