# The Origins and Evolution of Wardley Mapping: A Strategic Tool for Startups

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in startup environments, business strategy, and innovation roles

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the historical context and development of Wardley Mapping | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the unique advantages of Wardley Mapping for startup strategy | Evaluating |
| Create a basic Wardley Map for a startup scenario | Creating |
| Predict potential future developments in Wardley Mapping methodology | Evaluating |

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a startup scenario with complex strategic challenges. Ask learners to brainstorm how they would approach strategic planning for this startup using traditional methods.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in group discussion, share experiences with traditional strategic planning tools, identify limitations of current approaches.

**Resources Used:** Startup scenario description, whiteboard for capturing ideas

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with startup environments

**Technology Integration:** Use online polling tool to gather initial thoughts on strategic planning challenges

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce the concept of Wardley Mapping, its origins, and basic principles. Guide learners through the exploration of sample Wardley Maps.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze sample Wardley Maps, discuss observations in small groups, research additional information on Wardley Mapping online

**Resources Used:** Sample Wardley Maps, online resources on Wardley Mapping

**Differentiation:** Provide simplified and complex map examples to cater to different experience levels

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual breakout rooms for small group discussions, provide curated online resources for self-paced exploration

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Deliver a presentation on the evolution of Wardley Mapping, highlighting its three phases of development and applications in various sectors. Demonstrate the creation of a simple Wardley Map.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, participate in guided practice of creating a simple Wardley Map

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, interactive Wardley Mapping tool

**Differentiation:** Offer additional support for those struggling with map creation, provide advanced examples for quick learners

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing for demonstration, introduce online Wardley Mapping tools

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a case study analysis where learners apply Wardley Mapping to a real startup scenario. Encourage discussion on how Wardley Mapping addresses specific startup challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to create a Wardley Map for the case study, present findings to the class, discuss potential strategic decisions based on the map

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, collaborative mapping tools

**Differentiation:** Assign roles within groups based on expertise, provide additional guidance to groups as needed

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tools, virtual whiteboards for group work

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide a reflection session on the learning experience and potential applications of Wardley Mapping in learners' professional contexts. Administer a short assessment quiz.

**Learner Activities:** Share insights on how Wardley Mapping could be applied in their work, complete assessment quiz, set personal goals for further exploration of the methodology

**Resources Used:** Reflection prompts, online quiz platform

**Differentiation:** Offer choice in reflection format (written, verbal, or visual)

**Technology Integration:** Use online quiz platform for immediate feedback, digital goal-setting tool

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and map creation process
  - Alignment: Assesses understanding of Wardley Mapping principles and application skills
* **Summative**: Case study presentation and analysis
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to apply Wardley Mapping to real-world startup scenarios
* **Summative**: Short quiz on key concepts and historical development of Wardley Mapping
  - Alignment: Assesses knowledge retention and understanding of the methodology's evolution

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide additional scaffolding in map creation, offer simplified case studies
* **Experienced business professionals**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage peer teaching, focus on advanced applications
