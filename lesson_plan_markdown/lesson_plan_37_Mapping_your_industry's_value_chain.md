# Mapping Your Industry's Value Chain for Competitive Advantage

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, business strategists, and innovation managers

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze an industry's value chain using Wardley Mapping techniques | Analyzing |
| Evaluate inefficiencies and opportunities within a value chain | Evaluating |
| Create a strategic plan to leverage identified opportunities for competitive advantage | Creating |
| Predict potential future shifts in the value chain based on emerging technologies and trends | Evaluating |

## Key Concepts
* Value Chain Analysis
* Wardley Mapping
* Competitive Advantage
* Disruptive Innovation
* Vertical and Horizontal Integration
* Industry Evolution

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with industry analysis concepts
* General knowledge of their own industry landscape

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Digital whiteboard or collaborative mapping tool (e.g., Miro)
* Printed industry case studies
* Wardley Mapping templates

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief case study of a successful startup that disrupted an industry by identifying value chain inefficiencies. Facilitate a discussion on how the startup gained competitive advantage.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in discussion, sharing insights from their own industries and experiences with disruption.

**Resources Used:** Case study presentation, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide multiple case studies from different industries to cater to diverse professional backgrounds

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather initial thoughts on industry disruption

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce Wardley Mapping concepts and guide learners through the process of mapping a simple value chain. Provide examples and answer questions.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, practice creating a basic Wardley Map for a familiar industry, identifying key components and their evolution stages.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping guide, digital mapping tool

**Differentiation:** Offer additional support for those new to mapping concepts, provide advanced challenges for experienced strategists

**Technology Integration:** Utilize collaborative digital whiteboard for group mapping exercises

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Delve deeper into value chain analysis within the Wardley Mapping framework. Explain techniques for identifying inefficiencies, pain points, and opportunities for integration.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and begin to apply concepts to their own industry maps.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, real-world examples

**Differentiation:** Provide industry-specific examples to illustrate concepts for different sectors

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing to demonstrate advanced mapping techniques

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a comprehensive value chain mapping exercise for their own industries. Provide feedback and facilitate peer review sessions.

**Learner Activities:** Create detailed Wardley Maps for their industries, identify potential opportunities for disruption or competitive advantage, and develop initial strategic plans.

**Resources Used:** Digital mapping tools, strategic planning templates

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one coaching for those struggling with complex industries

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms for peer review sessions in virtual settings

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations of value chain analyses and strategic plans. Lead a discussion on potential future shifts in various industries.

**Learner Activities:** Present their value chain analyses and strategic plans to the group, provide constructive feedback to peers, and participate in discussions on future industry trends.

**Resources Used:** Presentation tools, feedback rubrics

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written) to accommodate different communication styles

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual presentation tools and collaborative feedback platforms

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during mapping exercises and group discussions
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping techniques and analyze value chains in real-time
* **Summative**: Final presentation of industry value chain analysis and strategic plan
  - Alignment: Evaluates learners' ability to synthesize concepts and create actionable strategies for their professional contexts

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide additional resources on basic strategy concepts, offer more structured templates for mapping exercises
* **Experienced business leaders**: Encourage deeper analysis of complex value chains, facilitate discussions on advanced strategic moves

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections

* Supply chain management

## Real-World Applications
* Identifying new market opportunities for startups
* Optimizing existing business processes for established companies
* Developing strategic partnerships based on value chain insights

* Conduct a more detailed analysis of a specific segment of the value chain
* Develop a pitch for a new startup idea based on identified opportunities
* Create a long-term strategic roadmap incorporating predicted value chain shifts

## Safety Considerations
* Remind participants about confidentiality when discussing specific company strategies
* Encourage ethical considerations when identifying opportunities for disruption

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How has this exercise changed your understanding of your industry's value chain?
* What potential opportunities or threats did you uncover that you hadn't considered before?
* How can you apply Wardley Mapping in your day-to-day strategic decision-making?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did the learners grasp and apply the Wardley Mapping concepts?
* What aspects of the lesson seemed to resonate most with the participants?
* How can the lesson be improved to better address the diverse needs of professional learners?
