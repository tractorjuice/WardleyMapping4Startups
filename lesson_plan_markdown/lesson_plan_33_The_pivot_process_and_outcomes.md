# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Successful Startup Pivots

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, product managers, and business strategists

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze a startup's current market position and value chain using Wardley Mapping | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the need for a pivot based on Wardley Map insights | Evaluating |
| Design alternative business scenarios using Wardley Mapping techniques | Creating |
| Formulate a pivot strategy based on Wardley Mapping analysis | Creating |
| Assess the outcomes of a pivot using Wardley Mapping as a measurement tool | Evaluating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Startup pivot
* Value chain analysis
* Market positioning
* Strategic decision-making
* Business model innovation
* Scenario planning

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup concepts and terminology
* General knowledge of market analysis techniques

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or online tool
* Whiteboard and markers
* Printed case study materials
* Post-it notes and pens

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of Startup X's initial challenges. Facilitate a discussion on common reasons startups need to pivot.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a think-pair-share activity to discuss personal experiences or knowledge of startup pivots.

**Resources Used:** Slide deck with Startup X overview, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with startup terminology

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital polling tool to gather and display learners' prior knowledge and experiences

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce Wardley Mapping concepts and guide learners through creating a basic Wardley Map for a hypothetical startup.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create a Wardley Map for the hypothetical startup using the provided online tool.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping tutorial video, online Wardley Mapping tool

**Differentiation:** Provide step-by-step instructions for novices, allow advanced learners to explore additional mapping features

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing to demonstrate Wardley Mapping techniques in real-time

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a detailed walkthrough of how Startup X used Wardley Mapping to identify the need for a pivot and explore alternatives.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze Startup X's initial and post-pivot Wardley Maps, identifying key differences and strategic implications.

**Resources Used:** Detailed case study materials, sample Wardley Maps from Startup X

**Differentiation:** Provide guiding questions for those who need more support in map analysis

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive digital maps to highlight key elements and changes in Startup X's strategy

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 75 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through applying Wardley Mapping to their own business scenarios or provided case studies.

**Learner Activities:** Create Wardley Maps for current and potential future states of a business, identify pivot opportunities, and develop a pivot strategy.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, business scenario templates

**Differentiation:** Offer choice in complexity of business scenarios based on learner experience

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online workspaces for group mapping exercises

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate peer review of pivot strategies and lead a discussion on lessons learned.

**Learner Activities:** Present pivot strategies to peers, provide and receive feedback, reflect on the application of Wardley Mapping to pivot decisions.

**Resources Used:** Peer review rubric, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written)

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital feedback tool for structured peer reviews

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and Wardley Mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final pivot strategy and Wardley Maps
  - Alignment: Measures ability to synthesize learning into a comprehensive pivot plan
* **Formative**: Peer review of pivot strategies
  - Alignment: Encourages critical thinking and application of learned concepts to various scenarios

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice startup founders**: Provide additional resources on basic business strategy concepts, offer more structured templates for Wardley Mapping exercises
* **Experienced business strategists**: Encourage exploration of advanced Wardley Mapping techniques, provide more complex business scenarios for analysis
