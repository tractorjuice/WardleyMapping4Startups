# Integrating Wardley Maps with Business Model Canvas for Strategic Innovation

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, business strategists, and innovation managers

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the synergies between Wardley Maps and Business Model Canvas for comprehensive strategic planning | Analyzing |
| Create an integrated Wardley Map and Business Model Canvas for a startup scenario | Creating |
| Evaluate the strategic implications of combining Wardley Maps with Business Model Canvas | Evaluating |
| Design an action plan to implement insights gained from the integrated approach | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Maps
* Business Model Canvas
* Strategic alignment
* Value chain visibility
* Competitive context
* Innovation opportunities
* Resource allocation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of Wardley Mapping
* Familiarity with Business Model Canvas
* General business strategy concepts

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Digital whiteboard tool (e.g., Miro, Mural)
* Wardley Mapping software
* Business Model Canvas templates
* Case study materials

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world startup scenario facing strategic challenges. Facilitate a brief discussion on how traditional tools might fall short in addressing these challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in group discussion, sharing experiences with strategic planning tools and their limitations.

**Resources Used:** Digital presentation of startup scenario

**Differentiation:** Provide multiple scenarios catering to different industries for learners to choose from based on their background.

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather initial thoughts on strategic planning approaches.

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a review of Wardley Mapping and Business Model Canvas individually. Introduce the concept of integration between the two tools.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create separate Wardley Maps and Business Model Canvases for the given scenario. Begin exploring potential connections between the two.

**Resources Used:** Digital Wardley Mapping tool, Business Model Canvas template

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support materials for those less familiar with either tool.

**Technology Integration:** Utilize collaborative digital workspaces for group activities.

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present the methodology for integrating Wardley Maps with Business Model Canvas. Demonstrate the process using a simplified example.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and discuss potential applications in their own professional contexts.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, integration methodology guide

**Differentiation:** Offer advanced concepts for experienced strategists, while providing foundational explanations for novices.

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing and annotation tools to enhance the demonstration.

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a hands-on workshop where learners apply the integrated approach to their scenario. Provide guidance and feedback throughout the process.

**Learner Activities:** Work in teams to create an integrated Wardley Map and Business Model Canvas for their startup scenario. Identify strategic insights and innovation opportunities.

**Resources Used:** Digital whiteboard tool, integration methodology guide

**Differentiation:** Assign roles within teams based on individual strengths and areas for development.

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms for team collaboration in virtual settings.

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Lead a session where teams present their integrated maps and canvases. Facilitate peer feedback and discussion on strategic implications.

**Learner Activities:** Present team findings, provide constructive feedback to peers, and reflect on the application of the integrated approach to their own professional contexts.

**Resources Used:** Digital presentation tools

**Differentiation:** Encourage various presentation formats to accommodate different communication preferences.

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual presentation tools with interactive features for audience engagement.

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during group activities and discussions
  - Alignment: Assesses progress towards analyzing and creating integrated strategic tools
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final integrated Wardley Map and Business Model Canvas presentation
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create and evaluate comprehensive strategic plans using the integrated approach
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on applying the integrated approach to a real-world professional challenge
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to transfer learning to authentic professional contexts

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide additional scaffolding materials, pair with more experienced peers, offer simplified scenarios
* **Experienced strategists**: Present more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of market dynamics, facilitate peer mentoring
