# Leveraging Wardley Maps for Effective Investor Communication

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, CEOs, and senior executives in technology-driven industries

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the strategic landscape of a startup using Wardley Mapping techniques | Analyzing |
| Create a simplified Wardley Map tailored for investor communication | Creating |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of using Wardley Maps in investor presentations | Evaluating |
| Design a compelling narrative around a Wardley Map to articulate startup vision and strategy | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Strategic positioning
* Market dynamics
* Investor communication
* Competitive advantage
* Value chain evolution
* Scenario planning

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup funding processes
* General knowledge of market analysis techniques

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or online tool
* Digital whiteboard for collaborative work
* Sample investor pitch decks
* Case study materials (e.g., TechInnovate)

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of the challenges in communicating startup vision to investors. Introduce the concept of Wardley Mapping as a solution.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a quick poll about their experiences with investor communications. Share challenges they've faced in articulating their startup's vision and strategy.

**Resources Used:** Interactive polling software, brief video introduction to Wardley Mapping

**Differentiation:** Provide optional pre-reading materials for those less familiar with Wardley Mapping

**Technology Integration:** Use an online polling tool to gather and display learner experiences in real-time

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through the process of creating a basic Wardley Map for their own startup or a provided case study.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to create a Wardley Map, identifying key components of their startup's value chain and positioning them on the map.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, digital collaboration tools

**Differentiation:** Provide templates and examples for different industry sectors

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tools for group work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present best practices for using Wardley Maps in investor communications. Analyze the TechInnovate case study.

**Learner Activities:** Discuss the key elements that made TechInnovate's investor communication successful. Identify how Wardley Maps contributed to their funding success.

**Resources Used:** Slide presentation, TechInnovate case study materials

**Differentiation:** Provide additional examples for different startup stages (seed, Series A, B, etc.)

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software with embedded case study materials

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop on refining Wardley Maps for investor presentations. Guide learners in creating multiple scenarios and integrating financial projections.

**Learner Activities:** Refine their group's Wardley Map, focusing on simplification and investor relevance. Develop a narrative around the map that highlights strategic foresight and adaptability.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, financial projection templates

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one coaching for groups struggling with map refinement

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing for group presentations and feedback

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Organize a mock investor pitch session. Provide constructive feedback on the use of Wardley Maps in presentations.

**Learner Activities:** Present their refined Wardley Maps and accompanying narratives in a simulated investor pitch. Peer-evaluate other groups' presentations.

**Resources Used:** Pitch evaluation rubric, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Allow for different presentation formats (e.g., slide deck, interactive demo)

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual breakout rooms for simultaneous pitch sessions

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing feedback during map creation and refinement process
  - Alignment: Aligns with objectives of analyzing strategic landscape and creating tailored maps
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final investor pitch presentation using Wardley Maps
  - Alignment: Aligns with objectives of creating compelling narratives and evaluating effectiveness of maps in presentations

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice startup founders**: Provide additional guidance on basic strategy concepts and Wardley Mapping techniques
* **Experienced executives**: Offer advanced scenarios and challenges to deepen their application of Wardley Mapping in complex business environments

* Preparing for actual investor meetings and funding rounds

* Develop a Wardley Map-based board presentation for the next strategic planning session

* Remind participants about confidentiality when sharing strategic information
* Discuss ethical considerations in presenting future scenarios to investors

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How has your perception of your startup's strategic position changed after creating a Wardley Map?
* In what ways do you think using Wardley Maps will enhance your next investor communication?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing this approach, and how might you overcome them?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did the learners grasp the concept of Wardley Mapping for investor communication?
* What aspects of the lesson seemed to resonate most with the participants?
* How can the lesson be improved to better address the diverse needs of startup founders and executives?
