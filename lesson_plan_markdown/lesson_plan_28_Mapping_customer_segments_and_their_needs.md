# Mapping Customer Segments and Their Needs: A Wardley Mapping Approach for Startups

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, product managers, and business strategists in the government and public sector

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze customer segments and their needs using Wardley Mapping techniques | Analyzing |
| Create a Wardley Map that visualizes customer segments, needs, and their relationships within the value chain | Creating |
| Evaluate strategic opportunities for innovation and growth based on the Wardley Map analysis | Evaluating |
| Design a customer development strategy that integrates Wardley Mapping principles | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Customer segments
* Customer needs
* Value chain
* Market evolution
* Strategic positioning
* Customer development
* Government and public sector innovation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with customer development concepts
* General knowledge of the government and public sector landscape

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or online tool (e.g., Wardley Maps Online)
* Whiteboard and markers
* Post-it notes
* Handouts with Wardley Mapping templates

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a case study of a public sector startup facing challenges in understanding and meeting customer needs. Facilitate a brief discussion on the importance of customer segmentation and needs analysis in the public sector context.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in the discussion, sharing experiences and challenges related to customer understanding in their own startups or projects.

**Resources Used:** Case study presentation, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide multiple examples of public sector startups to cater to diverse areas of interest

**Technology Integration:** Use an interactive polling tool to gather initial thoughts and experiences from participants

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce the concept of Wardley Mapping and its application to customer segmentation and needs analysis. Guide participants through a hands-on exercise to identify customer segments and needs for a hypothetical public sector startup.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to brainstorm and list customer segments and needs for the given scenario. Begin sketching a basic Wardley Map to visualize these elements.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping introduction slides, group exercise instructions, mapping templates

**Differentiation:** Provide additional guidance and simplified templates for those new to Wardley Mapping

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online whiteboards for group brainstorming and initial mapping

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Demonstrate the process of creating a comprehensive Wardley Map that includes customer segments, needs, and their relationships within the value chain. Explain the significance of positioning elements along the evolution axis.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and begin refining their group maps based on the demonstration and explanation.

**Resources Used:** Step-by-step Wardley Mapping demonstration, visual aids

**Differentiation:** Provide written instructions alongside the demonstration for different learning styles

**Technology Integration:** Use screen sharing and annotation tools to highlight key aspects of the mapping process

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants in applying Wardley Mapping to their own startup ideas or projects. Facilitate discussions on identifying strategic opportunities, potential pivots, and areas for innovation based on the maps.

**Learner Activities:** Create Wardley Maps for their own startups or projects, focusing on customer segments and needs. Analyze the maps to identify strategic insights and opportunities.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, guiding questions for analysis

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one coaching for participants who need additional support

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms for small group discussions and peer feedback on maps

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a gallery walk where participants present their Wardley Maps and insights. Lead a group discussion on key learnings and applications of Wardley Mapping in customer development.

**Learner Activities:** Present their Wardley Maps, explaining their analysis and strategic insights. Provide peer feedback and participate in the group discussion.

**Resources Used:** Presentation guidelines, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, written, visual) to accommodate different communication preferences

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital gallery platform for sharing and commenting on Wardley Maps

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group work and map creation process
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping techniques to customer segmentation and needs analysis
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Maps and strategic insights presentation
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create comprehensive Wardley Maps and derive strategic insights for startup development
* **Summative**: Written reflection on how Wardley Mapping will be applied to their startup or project
  - Alignment: Evaluates understanding of Wardley Mapping principles and ability to plan for real-world application

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice Wardley Mappers**: Provide simplified templates, step-by-step guides, and additional coaching during mapping exercises
* **Experienced strategists**: Offer advanced challenges, such as mapping multiple interconnected value chains or future scenario planning
* **Visual learners**: Emphasize the use of color-coding, icons, and visual aids in mapping exercises
* **Verbal learners**: Provide opportunities for verbal explanations and discussions of maps and concepts
