# Mapping the Value Chain for Your Startup: A Wardley Mapping Approach

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, entrepreneurs, and business strategists

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Create a comprehensive Wardley Map for a startup's value chain | Create |
| Analyze a Wardley Map to identify strategic insights and opportunities for innovation | Analyze |
| Evaluate the strategic implications of component positioning and dependencies in a Wardley Map | Evaluate |
| Apply Wardley Mapping principles to real-world startup scenarios | Apply |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Value Chain
* User Needs
* Component Dependencies
* Evolution Axis
* Strategic Analysis
* Innovation Opportunities

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of value chains and business models

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaborative platform
* Sticky notes or digital equivalent
* Markers or digital drawing tools
* Laptops or tablets for research and documentation
* Sample Wardley Maps for reference

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world startup case study with complex strategic challenges. Facilitate a brief discussion on how participants would approach analyzing and solving these challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in group discussion, brainstorm initial approaches to the case study

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide multiple case studies across different industries to allow choice based on interest or experience

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital polling tool to gather initial thoughts and display results in real-time

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce the concept of Wardley Mapping and its components. Guide participants through the process of identifying users, needs, and components for their own startups or the case study.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to identify users, needs, and components. Begin sketching initial value chain maps.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping guide, component identification worksheets

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support and examples for those new to the concept, allow advanced participants to work on more complex scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative mind-mapping tool to capture and organize ideas

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Demonstrate the process of creating a Wardley Map, including positioning components on the evolution axis and mapping dependencies. Explain the rationale behind each step.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and begin applying the demonstrated techniques to their own maps

**Resources Used:** Step-by-step Wardley Mapping guide, example maps from various industries

**Differentiation:** Offer one-on-one guidance for those struggling, provide advanced tips for quick learners

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital whiteboard to create and annotate example maps in real-time

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the process of refining their Wardley Maps, focusing on accurate positioning and identifying key dependencies. Facilitate discussions on strategic insights derived from the maps.

**Learner Activities:** Create detailed Wardley Maps for their startups or case studies, analyze maps for strategic insights, participate in peer reviews and discussions

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software or templates, strategic analysis framework

**Differentiation:** Provide additional challenges for advanced participants, such as mapping multiple scenarios or future states

**Technology Integration:** Use specialized Wardley Mapping software or online tools for creating and sharing maps

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations of Wardley Maps and derived strategic insights. Guide a reflective discussion on the process and its applications.

**Learner Activities:** Present Wardley Maps and strategic analyses to the group, provide peer feedback, reflect on the learning process and potential applications

**Resources Used:** Presentation tools, peer evaluation rubrics

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., formal presentations, gallery walks, or small group discussions)

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual breakout rooms for small group presentations and discussions in online settings

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during map creation process
  - Alignment: Aligns with the objective of creating a comprehensive Wardley Map, providing real-time guidance and correction
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final Wardley Maps and strategic analysis presentations
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create, analyze, and derive strategic insights from Wardley Maps, directly tied to learning objectives
* **Peer Assessment**: Structured peer review of Wardley Maps and strategic insights
  - Alignment: Encourages critical thinking and analysis skills, aligns with objectives of evaluating strategic implications

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice entrepreneurs**: Provide additional scaffolding, simplified examples, and more guided practice in map creation
* **Experienced business strategists**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of ecosystem dynamics and future evolution
* **Industry-specific professionals**: Provide industry-specific examples and encourage application to unique sector challenges

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Connect to project management for implementation of strategic insights
* Relate to marketing for understanding user needs and value propositions
* Link to finance for evaluating resource allocation based on map insights
* Connect to technology management for understanding component evolution

## Real-World Applications
* Use Wardley Maps to guide product development roadmaps
* Apply mapping techniques to identify potential partnerships or acquisition targets
* Utilize maps to communicate strategy to investors or board members
* Employ Wardley Mapping in competitive analysis and market positioning

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflect on how Wardley Mapping changes their approach to strategic thinking
* Consider how the mapping process reveals assumptions about their business model
* Analyze their decision-making process during map creation and strategic analysis

## Extension Activities

* Develop a series of maps showing potential future states of the industry
* Use Wardley Maps to guide a strategic planning session for the next quarter

* Ensure confidentiality of strategic information shared during the session
* Discuss ethical considerations in competitive analysis and strategy development
