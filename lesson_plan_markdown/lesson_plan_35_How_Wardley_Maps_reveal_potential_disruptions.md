# Leveraging Wardley Maps to Identify Disruptive Opportunities in Professional Contexts

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in technology strategy, business development, and government innovation roles

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the components of a Wardley Map to identify potential areas of disruption in a given industry or market | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the evolution patterns of components in a value chain to predict future disruptions | Evaluating |
| Create a Wardley Map for a specific business or government context to reveal potential disruptive opportunities | Creating |
| Synthesize insights from Wardley Maps to develop strategic recommendations for leveraging or responding to potential disruptions | Synthesizing |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Maps
* Value Chain
* Component Evolution
* Disruptive Innovation
* Weak Signal Detection
* Dependency Analysis
* Inertia Points
* Technological Shifts
* Market Gaps
* Competitive Landscape Visualization

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with value chain concepts
* General awareness of technological trends in their industry

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaborative platform
* Wardley Mapping software or templates
* Case studies of disruptive innovations in various industries
* Access to industry reports and trend analyses

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a recent case of industry disruption (e.g., cloud computing in government IT). Guide a discussion on how it could have been anticipated.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in group discussion, sharing experiences of disruption in their own industries.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, video clips of industry disruptions

**Differentiation:** Provide multiple examples from different sectors to cater to diverse professional backgrounds

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather initial thoughts on disruption prediction

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce Wardley Mapping concepts and demonstrate how to create a basic map. Guide learners through the process of mapping a simple value chain.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, create a draft Wardley Map for a familiar industry or organization.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping tutorial, sample maps from various industries

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support for those new to mapping, allow advanced learners to tackle more complex scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use online Wardley Mapping tools for collaborative mapping exercises

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Elaborate on how Wardley Maps reveal potential disruptions through evolution patterns, dependency analysis, and weak signal detection. Provide examples from the content.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze provided Wardley Maps to identify potential disruptions. Share insights with the larger group.

**Resources Used:** Detailed Wardley Maps from real-world scenarios, analysis framework

**Differentiation:** Offer varying levels of complexity in the maps provided for analysis

**Technology Integration:** Use digital annotation tools to highlight key areas of potential disruption on shared maps

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a complex case study related to government or public sector innovation. Guide learners in applying Wardley Mapping to identify disruptive opportunities.

**Learner Activities:** In teams, create a comprehensive Wardley Map for the case study, identify potential disruptions, and develop strategic recommendations.

**Resources Used:** Detailed case study materials, Wardley Mapping software

**Differentiation:** Assign roles within teams based on individual strengths and experiences

**Technology Integration:** Utilize collaborative online workspaces for team mapping and strategy development

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate team presentations of their Wardley Maps, disruption analyses, and strategic recommendations. Provide feedback and lead a reflective discussion.

**Learner Activities:** Present team findings, engage in peer evaluation, and participate in reflective discussion on the application of Wardley Mapping in their professional contexts.

**Resources Used:** Presentation tools, peer evaluation rubrics

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (e.g., verbal, visual, written) to accommodate different communication preferences

**Technology Integration:** Use digital feedback tools for real-time peer and facilitator evaluations

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Ongoing observation and feedback during mapping exercises and group discussions
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time, aligning with the analysis and evaluation objectives
* **Summative**: Team presentation of Wardley Map, disruption analysis, and strategic recommendations for the case study
  - Alignment: Evaluates learners' ability to synthesize insights and create strategic recommendations, aligning with higher-order learning objectives

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice professionals**: Provide additional guidance on Wardley Mapping techniques, offer simplified case studies, pair with more experienced learners
* **Experienced strategists**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of evolutionary patterns, facilitate peer teaching opportunities

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Link Wardley Mapping to change management strategies
* Explore connections with data science for trend analysis and prediction
* Discuss implications for policy-making and regulatory frameworks

## Real-World Applications
* Using Wardley Maps to inform technology investment decisions in organizations
* Applying mapping techniques to identify new market opportunities for startups
* Leveraging maps to anticipate and prepare for regulatory changes in government sectors

## Metacognition Opportunities
* Reflect on how Wardley Mapping challenges or enhances existing strategic thinking processes
* Consider how the mapping process reveals personal biases or assumptions about industry evolution
* Discuss how regular mapping practice could improve professional decision-making skills

## Extension Activities
* Create a Wardley Map for learners' own organizations and present findings to leadership
* Develop a 'weak signals' monitoring system based on insights from Wardley Mapping
* Conduct a workshop on Wardley Mapping for colleagues to spread the practice within the organization

## Safety Considerations
* Ensure confidentiality when discussing sensitive business information during exercises
* Address potential resistance to new strategic thinking methods and emphasize complementary use with existing tools

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How might Wardley Mapping change your approach to strategic planning in your role?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing Wardley Mapping in your organization, and how might you overcome them?
* How could regular use of Wardley Maps enhance your ability to anticipate and respond to industry disruptions?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did the lesson engage learners from diverse professional backgrounds?
* What aspects of Wardley Mapping seemed to resonate most with learners, and why?
* How could the lesson be improved to better address the practical challenges of implementing Wardley Mapping in various organizational contexts?
