# Disrupting Traditional Industries with Wardley Mapping: A Case Study of Startup Y

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in startup environments, business strategy, and innovation management

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze how Startup Y used Wardley Mapping to identify disruptive opportunities in the logistics industry | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of Wardley Mapping as a strategic tool for startup innovation | Evaluating |
| Create a basic Wardley Map for a potential disruptive opportunity in the learner's own industry | Creating |
| Apply lessons from Startup Y's experience to develop strategies for leveraging Wardley Mapping in various business contexts | Applying |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Disruptive innovation
* Value chain analysis
* Strategic decision-making
* Industry landscape visualization
* Technology integration
* Market positioning

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of supply chain and logistics concepts

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaborative space
* Wardley Mapping software or templates
* Case study materials on Startup Y
* Internet-connected devices for research

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of the logistics industry challenges and introduce Startup Y's success story. Pose thought-provoking questions about industry disruption.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion on personal experiences with logistics inefficiencies and brainstorm potential areas for innovation.

**Resources Used:** Slide deck with industry statistics and Startup Y introduction

**Differentiation:** Provide industry-specific examples for learners from diverse backgrounds

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather and display learners' ideas in real-time

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce Wardley Mapping concepts and guide learners through the process of creating a basic map.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, attempt to create a simplified Wardley Map of the traditional logistics industry based on provided information and personal knowledge.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping tutorial video, blank mapping templates

**Differentiation:** Provide additional guidance or advanced challenges based on learners' familiarity with mapping techniques

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online mapping tools for group work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a detailed analysis of Startup Y's Wardley Map and strategy. Highlight key decisions and their rationale.

**Learner Activities:** Compare their group's maps with Startup Y's actual map. Discuss differences and insights gained.

**Resources Used:** Detailed case study of Startup Y, including their Wardley Map

**Differentiation:** Offer additional context or simplification based on learners' industry knowledge

**Technology Integration:** Use annotation tools to highlight key elements of Startup Y's map during presentation

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop on applying Wardley Mapping to learners' own industries or business challenges.

**Learner Activities:** Individually or in pairs, create a Wardley Map for a potential disruptive opportunity in their own industry. Share and discuss with peers.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, industry research databases

**Differentiation:** Provide industry-specific guidance and resources for different sectors

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms in virtual settings for focused work and discussions

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide a reflection session on the learning experience and potential applications of Wardley Mapping in professional contexts.

**Learner Activities:** Complete a self-assessment questionnaire and participate in a final group discussion on key takeaways and action plans.

**Resources Used:** Self-assessment questionnaire, action plan template

**Differentiation:** Offer varied reflection prompts based on learners' roles and experience levels

**Technology Integration:** Use digital forms for self-assessment and collaborative documents for action planning

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses understanding of Wardley Mapping concepts and ability to apply them to real-world scenarios
* **Summative**: Evaluation of learner-created Wardley Maps for their own industries
  - Alignment: Measures ability to create and use Wardley Maps for strategic analysis in professional contexts
* **Summative**: Self-assessment questionnaire on learning objectives and confidence in applying Wardley Mapping
  - Alignment: Gauges learner perception of skill development and readiness to apply concepts in their work

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice professionals**: Provide additional explanations of industry-specific terms, offer more structured mapping templates
* **Experienced strategists**: Encourage deeper analysis, provide advanced readings on Wardley Mapping applications
* **Non-logistics professionals**: Offer analogies to other industries, focus on transferable strategic concepts
