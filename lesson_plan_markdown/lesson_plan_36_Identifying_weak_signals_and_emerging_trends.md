# Identifying Weak Signals and Emerging Trends Using Wardley Mapping

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in startup environments, particularly those working with government and public sector clients

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze Wardley Maps to identify potential weak signals and emerging trends in a given industry or market | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the impact of weak signals and emerging trends on current business strategies | Evaluating |
| Create a strategy to leverage identified weak signals and emerging trends for competitive advantage | Creating |
| Apply Wardley Mapping techniques to detect early indicators of change in professional contexts | Applying |

## Key Concepts
* Weak signals
* Emerging trends
* Wardley Mapping
* Component evolution
* Ecosystem mapping
* Cross-industry comparison
* Inertia points
* Technological advancements
* Disruption
* Innovation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of Wardley Mapping concepts
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of business strategy

## Materials Needed
* Wardley Mapping software or large sheets of paper and markers
* Case study materials
* Access to industry reports and trend analysis tools
* Laptops or tablets for research and collaborative work

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a recent case of disruption in the government/public sector. Guide a discussion on how it could have been anticipated.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in discussion, sharing experiences of unexpected market changes in their professional contexts.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, video clip of a disruptive innovation

**Differentiation:** Provide multiple examples from different sectors to cater to diverse professional backgrounds

**Technology Integration:** Use an interactive polling tool to gather learners' initial thoughts on weak signals

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce key concepts of weak signals and emerging trends. Demonstrate how to use Wardley Mapping for identification.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, analyze provided Wardley Maps to identify potential weak signals and emerging trends.

**Resources Used:** Sample Wardley Maps, guided worksheet for analysis

**Differentiation:** Provide maps of varying complexity to match different experience levels

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online whiteboarding tools for group map analysis

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Elaborate on the five key areas for identifying weak signals (Component Evolution, Ecosystem Mapping, etc.). Provide real-world examples.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and relate concepts to their own professional experiences.

**Resources Used:** Detailed slides on key areas, industry case studies

**Differentiation:** Offer additional reading materials for those wanting to delve deeper

**Technology Integration:** Use a learning management system to provide supplementary resources

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a practical exercise of creating a Wardley Map for their own industry/organization and identifying weak signals.

**Learner Activities:** Create Wardley Maps, identify potential weak signals, and develop strategies to leverage them.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software or materials, strategy development template

**Differentiation:** Provide one-on-one coaching for those struggling with the mapping process

**Technology Integration:** Use breakout rooms in virtual settings for small group collaboration

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate group presentations of identified weak signals and strategies. Provide feedback and lead a reflective discussion.

**Learner Activities:** Present findings, provide peer feedback, and reflect on the application of learned concepts to their work.

**Resources Used:** Presentation guidelines, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Allow for various presentation formats (verbal, visual, written) to accommodate different strengths

**Technology Integration:** Use a digital platform for peer feedback and assessment

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and map analysis activities
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in identifying weak signals
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final presentations on identified weak signals and strategies
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to analyze, evaluate, and create strategies based on identified trends
* **Formative**: Self-assessment questionnaire on understanding of key concepts
  - Alignment: Allows learners to reflect on their grasp of theoretical concepts and identify areas for improvement

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice professionals**: Provide additional guidance on Wardley Mapping basics, offer simplified industry examples
* **Experienced professionals**: Encourage deeper analysis, provide more complex scenarios, facilitate peer teaching opportunities
* **Visual learners**: Emphasize use of Wardley Maps and visual representations of trends
* **Verbal learners**: Provide opportunities for discussion and verbal explanation of concepts
