# Mastering Wardley Mapping for Strategic Innovation in Startups

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, executives, and strategic planners in technology and innovation sectors

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the components and structure of a Wardley Map | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the applicability of Wardley Mapping to specific startup scenarios | Evaluating |
| Create a basic Wardley Map for a given startup scenario | Creating |
| Synthesize insights from a Wardley Map to inform strategic decision-making | Synthesizing |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Value Chain
* Evolution Axis
* Strategic Visualization
* Component Interdependencies
* Market Anticipation
* Resource Optimization
* Innovation Focus

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of value chains and market evolution

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital collaborative platform
* Wardley Mapping software or templates
* Case studies of successful Wardley Map applications
* Handouts on Wardley Mapping principles

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a complex startup scenario and challenge participants to articulate their strategic approach. Introduce Wardley Mapping as a solution to the limitations they encounter.

**Learner Activities:** Discuss in small groups the challenges of strategic planning in startups. Share experiences with traditional methods.

**Resources Used:** Startup scenario handout, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide varied scenarios catering to different industry sectors

**Technology Integration:** Use online polling tool for real-time feedback on strategic challenges

### Explore
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the basic components of a Wardley Map using a simple example. Facilitate a group exercise in identifying components and their positions on the map.

**Learner Activities:** In pairs, practice creating a simple Wardley Map for a familiar business process or product.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, step-by-step guide

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support or advanced challenges based on participants' grasp of the concept

**Technology Integration:** Utilize collaborative online mapping tools for pair work

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Deliver a comprehensive explanation of Wardley Mapping principles, including value chain analysis, evolution axis, and strategic implications. Use real-world examples from successful startups.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes, ask questions, and participate in discussions about the application of Wardley Mapping in various startup contexts.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, case studies

**Differentiation:** Offer additional readings for those seeking deeper understanding

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software with embedded quizzes

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a complex startup case study. Guide participants in applying Wardley Mapping to analyze the scenario and develop strategic recommendations.

**Learner Activities:** Work in small groups to create a comprehensive Wardley Map for the case study. Develop and present strategic recommendations based on the map.

**Resources Used:** Case study materials, Wardley Mapping software

**Differentiation:** Assign roles within groups based on individual strengths and experiences

**Technology Integration:** Use virtual breakout rooms for group work and digital presentation tools for sharing results

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a reflection session on the learning process and the potential applications of Wardley Mapping in participants' own startups.

**Learner Activities:** Complete a self-assessment questionnaire. Share insights on how they plan to implement Wardley Mapping in their work.

**Resources Used:** Self-assessment questionnaire, reflection prompts

**Differentiation:** Offer personalized feedback based on individual responses

**Technology Integration:** Use online forms for self-assessment and a digital whiteboard for sharing implementation ideas

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of pair and group work during Explore and Elaborate phases
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts in real-time
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final group presentations of Wardley Maps and strategic recommendations
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to create and interpret Wardley Maps for complex scenarios
* **Summative**: Individual reflection paper on applying Wardley Mapping to their own startup or business idea
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to transfer learning to personal professional context

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategic planners**: Provide additional scaffolding in map creation, offer simplified scenarios
* **Experienced executives**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage peer mentoring, focus on advanced applications
