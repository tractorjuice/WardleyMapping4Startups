# Integrating Wardley Mapping with Startup Methodologies for Strategic Innovation

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, innovation managers, and public sector leaders

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the synergies between Wardley Mapping and other startup methodologies | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of integrating Wardley Mapping with Lean Startup, Agile, and Design Thinking approaches | Evaluating |
| Create an integrated strategy using Wardley Mapping and other methodologies for a public sector innovation project | Creating |
| Justify the use of an integrated approach for navigating complex innovation landscapes | Evaluating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Lean Startup
* Agile Development
* Design Thinking
* Business Model Canvas
* Value Chain Visualization
* Strategic Alignment
* Public Sector Innovation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of startup methodologies
* Familiarity with strategic planning concepts
* Experience in innovation or entrepreneurship

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard and markers
* Laptops or tablets for participants
* Wardley Mapping software or templates
* Case study materials
* Handouts on methodology integration

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world public sector innovation challenge. Facilitate a discussion on the limitations of using single methodologies.

**Learner Activities:** Brainstorm potential approaches to the challenge using familiar methodologies.

**Resources Used:** Slide deck with case study, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide context-specific examples for different sectors represented in the audience

**Technology Integration:** Use digital whiteboard for collaborative brainstorming

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Introduce Wardley Mapping and guide participants through creating a basic map for the case study.

**Learner Activities:** Create individual Wardley Maps, then compare and discuss in small groups.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping tutorial video, mapping templates

**Differentiation:** Provide additional support for those new to Wardley Mapping

**Technology Integration:** Use Wardley Mapping software for interactive map creation

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present the concept of integrating Wardley Mapping with other methodologies. Demonstrate examples of successful integration.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze provided examples and discuss potential benefits and challenges of integration.

**Resources Used:** Slide deck with integration examples, discussion guide

**Differentiation:** Offer advanced examples for experienced practitioners

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software for real-time polling and feedback

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 75 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants in applying the integrated approach to their own professional contexts.

**Learner Activities:** Develop an integrated strategy for a real or hypothetical project, incorporating Wardley Mapping with at least one other methodology.

**Resources Used:** Strategy development worksheet, peer review rubric

**Differentiation:** Allow choice of project complexity based on experience level

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative project management tools for strategy development

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate peer presentations and provide expert feedback on integrated strategies.

**Learner Activities:** Present strategies to peers, engage in constructive critique, and reflect on learning.

**Resources Used:** Presentation guidelines, feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Offer optional advanced critique for experienced participants

**Technology Integration:** Use video conferencing breakout rooms for small group presentations

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Peer review of Wardley Maps created during the Explore phase
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts to real-world scenarios
* **Summative**: Evaluation of final integrated strategy presentations
  - Alignment: Measures ability to synthesize methodologies and apply to professional contexts

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice innovators**: Provide additional scaffolding for Wardley Mapping, offer simplified integration examples
* **Experienced strategists**: Challenge with complex scenarios, encourage mentoring of less experienced peers

## Cross-Disciplinary Connections
* Explore applications in project management
* Discuss implications for organizational change management
* Consider relevance to policy development processes

* Conduct a mini-workshop on integrated methodologies within participants' organizations
* Develop a case study of applying the integrated approach to a current work project

## Safety Considerations
* Ensure confidentiality when discussing sensitive organizational strategies
* Address potential resistance to new methodologies in traditional organizational cultures

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How does integrating Wardley Mapping enhance your current strategic planning process?
* What challenges do you anticipate in implementing this integrated approach in your organization?
* How might this approach impact decision-making and innovation in your professional context?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did the lesson address the diverse experience levels of participants?
* What aspects of the integration concept seemed to resonate most with learners?
* How can the lesson be improved to better meet the needs of public sector innovators?
