# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Enhanced Customer Interviews and Feedback Sessions

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Startup founders, product managers, and customer development professionals

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the components of a Wardley Map to identify key areas for customer exploration | Analyzing |
| Design targeted interview questions based on Wardley Map insights | Creating |
| Evaluate customer feedback in the context of value chain dynamics | Evaluating |
| Synthesize customer insights to iterate on business models and strategies | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Customer Development
* Value Chain Analysis
* Component Evolution
* Strategic Positioning
* Lean Startup Principles

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business models
* Familiarity with customer interview techniques
* General knowledge of product development processes

## Materials Needed
* Whiteboard or digital mapping tool
* Sample Wardley Maps
* Customer interview templates
* Case study materials
* Laptops or tablets for participants

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a real-world scenario of a startup struggling with customer development. Introduce Wardley Mapping as a potential solution.

**Learner Activities:** Discuss in small groups the challenges in the scenario and brainstorm potential approaches.

**Resources Used:** Scenario description, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with startup challenges

**Technology Integration:** Use a collaborative digital whiteboard for group brainstorming

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants through the creation of a basic Wardley Map for the scenario startup. Demonstrate how to identify key components and their evolution stages.

**Learner Activities:** Create individual Wardley Maps, focusing on customer needs and potential pain points.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping templates, component evolution guidelines

**Differentiation:** Offer simplified mapping templates for beginners, advanced options for experienced participants

**Technology Integration:** Utilize online Wardley Mapping tools for interactive map creation

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present the key aspects of using Wardley Maps in customer interviews, including identifying assumptions and exploring value chain dynamics.

**Learner Activities:** Analyze sample Wardley Maps to identify potential interview focus areas and questions.

**Resources Used:** Presentation slides, sample Wardley Maps

**Differentiation:** Provide additional examples for those struggling with map interpretation

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive presentation software for real-time polling and Q&A

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a role-playing exercise where participants conduct mock customer interviews using their Wardley Maps.

**Learner Activities:** In pairs, take turns being the interviewer and customer, using Wardley Maps to guide the conversation and record insights.

**Resources Used:** Interview guidelines, feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Adjust role-play scenarios based on participants' industry experience

**Technology Integration:** Use video conferencing breakout rooms for virtual role-playing

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide participants in reflecting on their experience and updating their Wardley Maps based on mock interview insights.

**Learner Activities:** Present updated maps and key learnings to the group, discuss potential business model iterations.

**Resources Used:** Reflection prompts, presentation guidelines

**Differentiation:** Offer additional support for synthesizing insights to less experienced participants

**Technology Integration:** Use digital annotation tools for collaborative map review

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of map creation and role-playing exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses ability to apply Wardley Mapping concepts to customer interview scenarios
* **Summative**: Presentation of updated Wardley Map and proposed business model iterations
  - Alignment: Evaluates synthesis of customer insights and strategic thinking skills

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice professionals**: Provide additional guidance on Wardley Mapping basics, offer simplified templates and extra examples
* **Experienced professionals**: Challenge with more complex scenarios, encourage deeper exploration of value chain dynamics

* Remind participants of ethical considerations in customer interviews

## Reflection Questions
### For Learners
* How has Wardley Mapping changed your perspective on customer development?
* What challenges did you face in applying Wardley Map insights to customer interviews?
* How might you integrate this approach into your current work processes?

### For Facilitator
* How effectively did participants grasp the connection between Wardley Mapping and customer interviews?
* What aspects of the lesson seemed most valuable to participants?
* How can the role-playing exercise be improved for future sessions?
