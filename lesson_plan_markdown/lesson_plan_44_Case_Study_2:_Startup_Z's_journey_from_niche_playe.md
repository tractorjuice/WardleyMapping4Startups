# Leveraging Wardley Mapping for Strategic Innovation: Startup Z's Journey to Market Leadership

**Duration:** 4 hours
**Target Audience:** Professional learners in startup management, business strategy, and technology innovation roles

## Learning Objectives

| Objective | Bloom's Taxonomy Level |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Analyze the key stages of Startup Z's journey using Wardley Mapping principles | Analyzing |
| Evaluate the effectiveness of Wardley Mapping in identifying disruptive opportunities | Evaluating |
| Create a basic Wardley Map for a given business scenario | Creating |
| Propose strategic decisions based on insights from Wardley Mapping | Creating |

## Key Concepts
* Wardley Mapping
* Strategic pivot
* Disruptive innovation
* Market analysis
* Ecosystem mapping
* Product development strategy
* Scaling strategy
* Continuous innovation

## Prior Knowledge
* Basic understanding of business strategy
* Familiarity with startup ecosystems
* General knowledge of technology trends

## Materials Needed
* Laptops or tablets with internet access
* Wardley Mapping software or large sheets of paper and markers
* Case study materials on Startup Z
* Handouts on Wardley Mapping principles

## Lesson Structure
### Engage
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a brief overview of Startup Z's success story and introduce the concept of Wardley Mapping. Pose thought-provoking questions about strategic decision-making in startups.

**Learner Activities:** Participate in a group discussion on challenges faced by startups in identifying market opportunities. Share personal experiences or known cases of successful pivots.

**Resources Used:** Slide deck with Startup Z overview, discussion prompts

**Differentiation:** Provide additional context for those less familiar with startup ecosystems

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive polling software to gather initial thoughts on strategic decision-making

### Explore
**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through the basics of Wardley Mapping using simplified examples. Introduce the key stages of Startup Z's journey.

**Learner Activities:** In small groups, practice creating simple Wardley Maps for familiar industries. Begin analyzing Startup Z's initial market position using provided data.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping tutorial videos, worksheets with industry scenarios

**Differentiation:** Provide more detailed guidance for novice mappers, challenge experienced learners with complex scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use online Wardley Mapping tools for hands-on practice

### Explain
**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Present a detailed analysis of Startup Z's Wardley Map at different stages of their journey. Explain how the map informed their strategic decisions.

**Learner Activities:** Take notes and ask questions. Participate in a guided discussion on the insights revealed by Startup Z's Wardley Maps.

**Resources Used:** Detailed case study materials, expert commentary videos

**Differentiation:** Provide additional explanations for complex concepts, offer advanced readings for those grasping quickly

**Technology Integration:** Use interactive Wardley Maps to demonstrate evolution over time

### Elaborate
**Duration:** 75 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Facilitate a workshop where learners apply Wardley Mapping to their own business scenarios or to provided case studies.

**Learner Activities:** Create Wardley Maps for given scenarios or their own business contexts. Identify potential disruptive opportunities and propose strategic actions.

**Resources Used:** Wardley Mapping software, business scenario cards

**Differentiation:** Offer more structured templates for beginners, allow advanced learners to tackle more complex scenarios

**Technology Integration:** Use collaborative online whiteboards for group mapping exercises

### Evaluate
**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Facilitator Actions:** Guide learners through a reflection process on their Wardley Mapping experience. Facilitate peer feedback on proposed strategies.

**Learner Activities:** Present their Wardley Maps and strategic proposals to peers. Provide and receive constructive feedback.

**Resources Used:** Evaluation rubrics, peer feedback forms

**Differentiation:** Adjust presentation format based on learner comfort (e.g., written, oral, or visual)

**Technology Integration:** Use digital portfolios to showcase final Wardley Maps and proposals

## Assessment Methods
* **Formative**: Observation of group discussions and mapping exercises
  - Alignment: Assesses understanding of Wardley Mapping principles and application to real scenarios
* **Summative**: Final presentation of a Wardley Map and strategic proposal for a given business scenario
  - Alignment: Evaluates ability to create and interpret Wardley Maps, and propose data-driven strategic decisions

## Differentiation Strategies
* **Novice strategists**: Provide more structured templates, offer additional guidance on mapping techniques, focus on simpler business scenarios
* **Experienced business leaders**: Offer more complex scenarios, encourage deeper analysis of market dynamics, facilitate peer-to-peer knowledge sharing
